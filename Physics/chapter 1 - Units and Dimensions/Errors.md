
Measured Values - a$_1$,a$_2$,a$_3$...
absolute error($\Delta$a):
$$\Delta a_1 = |\Delta a - a_1|$$
mean of absolute error:
$$
\Delta a_m = \frac{\Delta a_1 + \Delta a_2 + \Delta a_n}{n}$$
Relative/fractional error :
$$\frac {\Delta a_{m}}{\Delta a} $$
% error = relative error x 100

- errors are always added
	- 9.34 $\pm$ 0.34 -> A
	- 8.3 $\pm$ 1.09 -> B
	- A-B = 1.04 $\pm$  1.43

### Types of errors:
1- Systematic
	i)Experimental Condition
	ii)Instrumental
	iii)Personal Bias
2-Random

### least count error
- Smallest measurement possible by a device, is least count. So least count error will be in the order of the smallest measurement possible
- the discrepancy between measured value and true value is called error
- error - |true value - recorded value|

**Q:** Time for 20 oscillations of a pendulum is measured as t1=39.6s,t2=39.9s,t3=39.5. What is the precision in the measurements ? What is the accuracy of the measurement ?


**Answer:**

**Step 1: Calculate the Precision**  
Precision is defined as the smallest unit of measurement that can be reliably read from the measuring instrument. In this case, we can determine the precision by looking at the readings.

**Given Readings:**  
- t1=39.6s  
- t2=39.9s  
- t3=39.5s

The smallest increment between the readings is 0.1s (for example, from 39.6 to 39.7). Thus, the precision of the measurements is:

Precision=0.1s

**Step 2: Calculate the Mean Value of the Measurements**  
Next, we calculate the mean (average) of the three measurements to find the central tendency.

Tmean=t1+t2+t33=39.6+39.9+39.53

Calculating this gives:

Tmean=119.03=39.7s

**Step 3: Calculate the Absolute Errors**  
Now, we will find the absolute errors for each measurement by subtracting the mean value from each individual measurement.

1. For t1:  
Absolute Error1=|t1−Tmean|=|39.6−39.7|=0.1s

2. For t2:  
Absolute Error2=|t2−Tmean|=|39.9−39.7|=0.2s

3. For t3:  
Absolute Error3=|t3−Tmean|=|39.5−39.7|=0.2s

**Step 4: Calculate the Mean Absolute Error**  
Now, we find the mean of these absolute errors to determine the accuracy.

Mean Absolute Error=Absolute Error1+Absolute Error2+Absolute Error33=0.1+0.2+0.23

Calculating this gives:

Mean Absolute Error=0.53≈0.1667s 

$Precision = 0.1s$
$Accuracy=\pm 0.2s$
