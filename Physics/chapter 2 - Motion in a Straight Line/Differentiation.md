Differentiation is used for finding instantaneous rate of change of a function. It is a method to find the derivative of the function. The most common example is the rate of change of displacement with respect to time, called velocity.

## important formulas
$$\frac {d} {dx} (x^n) = nx^{n-1} $$
$$\frac d {dx} (\ln x) = \frac 1 x $$
$$\frac d {dx} (e^x) = e^x $$
$$\frac d {dx} (C) = 0 $$
$$\frac d {dx} (\sin x) = \cos x $$
$$\frac d {dx}(\cos x) = -\sin x$$
$$\frac d {dx} (\tan x) = \sec^2x$$
$$\frac d {dx} (uv) = \frac {udv} {dx} + \frac{vdu} {dx}$$
