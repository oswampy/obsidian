

Sound wave is a mechanical vibration that travels in all mediums and carries energy from one point to another.

## Properties:

1. needs material medium
2. longitudinal in nature
3. speed of sound at NTP is 332 m/s
4. audible range is 20Hz to 20kHz
5. cannot be polarized

## Propagation of Sound wave

i) particle move in direction of velocity of sound

ii) each particle will do SHM about its mean position

iii) vibration in air column in organ pipe are longitudinal in nature

## Velocity of Sound

### Newton's Formulas

#### a) For Solid medium

$V = \sqrt{\frac{Y}{\rho}}$ Y- Young's modulus

experimental V = 5150 m/s

#### b) for liquid medium

$V = \sqrt{\frac{B}{\rho}}$ B- Bulk modulus or volume elasticity

experimental V = 1412 m/s

#### c) For gas medium:

$V = \sqrt{\frac{E}{\rho}}$ E - elastic coefficient of medium

Newton assumed Isothermal process so might be no miscalculated

$V = \sqrt{\frac{P}{\rho}}$

experimental V = 237 m/s

---

correct eq<sup>n</sup> given by laplace:

$$V = \sqrt{\frac{\gamma P}{\rho}}$$

### Effect of relative density:

when humidity increases, density will be less (as H₂O occupies N₂, O₂) and so velocity will increase.

### Effect of pressure

$V = \sqrt{\frac{E}{\rho}} = \sqrt{\frac{\gamma RT}{ M}}$

Pressure has no effect on velocity of sound until temp changes

### Effect of velocity of air:

If air is bet blowing in direction of sound with speed w. The speed will be $(V + W)$

If in opposite direction of wind The $(V - W)$

**frequency has no impact on velocity of sound i.e sound waves of diff frequency travel with same speed in air**

 #Q) 
 A piezo electric quartz plate of thickness 0.005 m is vibrating in resonant condition

Find its fundamental frequency if

$Y = 8 \times 10^{10}$ N/m² $\rho = 2.65 \times 10^3$ kg/m³
 Solution:

$V = \sqrt{\frac{Y}{\rho}} = \sqrt{\frac{8 \times 10^{10}}{2.65 \times 10^3}} = 5.5 \times 10^3$ m/s

$\frac{\lambda}{2} = d$
$\lambda = 2 \times 5 \times 10^{-3} = 10^{-2}$ m
$V = f\lambda$

$f = \frac{V}{\lambda} = \frac{5.5 \times 10^3}{1 \times 10^{-2}}$

$f = 5.5 \times 10^5$ Hz = 550 kHz

# Characteristics of Sound wave

- **Pitch:** It is the characteristic that depends on fundamental frequency of sound. $\Rightarrow$ High pitch means High frequency
    
- **Loudness:** refers to strength of auditory sensation produced by sound. $\Rightarrow$ It depends on Intensity and Amplitude of frequency
    
    Loudness $= \log_e I$ $= Bel(\frac{I_0 = \text{ reference Intensity}}{I_0 = 10^{-12} \text{ watt/m}^2})$
    
    L = 10 log₁₀ $\frac{I}{I_0}$ decibel
    
    1 bel = 10 decibel
    Sound Levels:
	    Minimum audible sound = 0 dB 
	    Whispering (1m) = 10dB 
	    Normal talk (1m) = 60dB
	    Maximum tolerable Sound = 120 dB
    

---

- **Quality of Sound:** The characteristic which differentiate sound coming from different sources of frequencies
    
- **Musical Interval**
    The ratio of frequencies of two notes sounded together is called their musical interval: $\frac{F_1}{F_2}$
    
    Unison $\Rightarrow$ 1:1 octave $\Rightarrow$ 2:1 major Tone $\Rightarrow$ 9:8 minor Tone $\Rightarrow$ 10:9 Semitone $\Rightarrow$ 16:15
    

$\Rightarrow$ Infrasonic frequency < 20 Hz

$\Rightarrow$ Ultrasonic frequency > 20kHz

$\Rightarrow$ Supersonic Velocity Velocity greater than velocity of sound in local region
