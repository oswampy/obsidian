
## Definition

**Wave:** A wave is a disturbance which propagates energy from place to place without transporting matter.

→ Wave cannot be said to be localized  
→ It is spread over region or space without clear cut boundaries

## Classification of Waves Based On:

### 1) Medium

**a) Elastic/mechanical wave** - wave which requires material medium for propagation  
eg: Sound/water wave

**b) EM wave** - which requires no material medium for propagation  
eg: light, x-ray, infrared, etc

---

### 2) Vibration

**a) Transverse wave** - wave in which particles vibrates in perpendicular direction of its propagation  
eg: EM wave, wave on string

### 3) Dimensions

1-D, 2-D, 3-D

### 4) Propagation of Energy

i) Progressive wave  
ii) Stationary wave

---

## Terms Related to Waves

### i) Amplitude (A):

Maximum displacement of vibrating particle from mean position.

### ii) Time Period (T):

Time taken to complete one oscillation

### iii) Wave frequency (ν):

No. of vibrations made per second made by the particle

---

**Note:** Particles are said to be in phase if the angle between them is 0° or they are moving in same direction at same time.

### iv) Wavelength (λ):

Distance between two consecutive particles in same phase

```
    Y ↑
      |     A       B       C
    --+---------------------------→ x
      O
```

(O,B) or (A,C) can be used to measure wavelength

### If velocity of wave (not particle) is V

$$\lambda = VT, \quad V = \frac{\lambda}{T} \Rightarrow V = \lambda n$$

---

## Plane Progressive Wave

A wave that travels continuously in a specific direction through a medium

$$y = A\sin(kx - \omega t + \phi)$$

**Wave number (k):** no. of waves per unit distance. $$k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$$

If $x = \lambda$ and $t = T$  
$$y = A\sin(\phi)$$

$\phi$ have information about $t=0$, $x=0$ displacement

$\phi$ is phase angle at $x=0$, $t=0$

### Wave Diagram Analysis

```
    Y ↑     2     6

      |       4   8
```

$$y_1 = a\sin(\omega t)$$

time taken by wave to reach particle 6:  
$$z = \frac{x}{V}$$

At any time $t$, particle 1 is vibrating from $t$ time but particle 6 is vibrating from $(t-z)$

$$y_6 = A\sin(\omega(t-z))$$

$$y_6 = A\sin\left(\frac{2\pi t}{T} - \frac{2\pi x}{TV}\right)$$

$$y_6 = A\sin\left(\frac{2\pi}{T}\left(t - \frac{x}{V}\right)\right)$$

$$y_6 = A\sin(\omega t - kx)$$

$$V_w = n\lambda = \frac{\lambda \times 2\pi}{2\pi} = \frac{\omega}{k}$$ (wave velocity)

$$y = A\sin(\omega t - kx)$$

If we take $\frac{dy}{dt}$ w.r.t. time, we will get velocity of any particle at time $t$. If we take $\frac{dy}{dx}$ w.r.t. $x$, we get velocity of wave


## Velocity of Wave

$$V_w = \frac{dy}{dx} = -ak\cos(\omega t - kx)$$

$x$ describes the position of wave  
where $\omega t$ describes position of particle

$$V_p = \frac{dy}{dt} = a\omega\cos(\omega t - kx)$$

$$\frac{V_p}{V_w} = \frac{dy/dt}{dy/dx} = \frac{-\omega}{k} = -V_w$$

$$\frac{dy}{dt} = -V_w \times \frac{dy}{dx}$$

$$V_p = -V_w \times \text{slope of curve}$$
