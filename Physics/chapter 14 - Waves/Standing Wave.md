

When progressive wave and reflected wave superimpose each other, it forms stationary wave or standing wave.

---

## Types of Standing Wave

**i) Transverse standing wave on stretched string/wire**

When a wire is clamped to rigid supports at its ends and plucked in the middle to transverse progressive wave travels towards both ends of wire. Velocity of these waves is given by:

$$V = \sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}}$$

(T is tension, μ is linear mass density)

$$\Rightarrow \rho \Rightarrow \frac{M}{LA} = \ , \quad V = \sqrt{\frac{T}{\rho A}}$$

Thick wire μ ↓ So V↑

---

**1)** If T is constant: $V \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu}}$ or $V \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{m}}$

**2)** If μ is const: $V \propto \sqrt{T}$

**3)** If T is const and wire of diff radius/area: $V \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{s}}$

**4)** If T is const. and wire of same radius: $T \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{s}}$

---

• When progressive wave is generated on stretched wire after reflection from its ends, it will superimpose to produce transverse standing wave.

**If wire is clamped at both ends and plucked at middle of wire, then we'll have nodes at both ends and antinode in middle.**

If distance between 2 nodes is L, then:

$$L = \frac{\lambda}{2}$$

$$\lambda = 2L$$ (wavelength depends length)

$$V = f\lambda$$

$$\sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}} = f \cdot 2L$$

$$f = \frac{1}{2L}\sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}}$$ - Fundamental frequency; when double clamped wire is plucked in middle

---

**→ If we want vibration of different frequency then touch the wire with soft object like feather f and pluckin at different point. This will create a node where feather plucks.**

$$f = \frac{1}{L}\sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}}$$

frequency in this case is twice the frequency of fundamental frequency.

---

**- General case**

If we touch wire at length $\frac{L}{n}$ of wire:

$$x = \frac{L}{n} \Rightarrow \lambda = \frac{2L}{n}$$

$$V = \frac{n}{2L}\sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}}$$

---

**1)** $f_1 = \frac{1}{2L}\sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}}$ → fundamental tone → first harmonic

$f_2 = \frac{1}{L}\sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}}$ → first overtone → 2nd harmonic

$f_3 = \frac{3}{2L}\sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}}$ → 2nd overtone → 3rd harmonic

⋮

$f_n = \frac{n}{2L}\sqrt{\frac{T}{\mu}} \Rightarrow (n-1)^{th}$ overtone → $n^{th}$ harmonic
