## Thermodynamic System

Collection of extremely large no. of atoms and molecules confined within certain boundaries such that it has certain value of pressure, temperature, volume is called thermodynamical system.

## Three types are:

1. Open system: where mass and Energy can be exchanged with surr.
    
2. Closed system: only energy, not mass can be exchanged
    
3. Isolated system: no exchange of mass nor energy.
    

---

**Note:** T and P of surr. is always const.

## State of System

the properties which will give information and physical behaviour of system is called state of system.

**State variable:** Physical properties which only depend on initial or state of system not path.

**Path variable:** Physical properties which depend on path taken by system to reach that state.

##  First Law of Thermodynamics

Statement: If a system absorbs $d Q$ amount of heat from the surrounding, as a result $d U$ changes by $d U$ and the system does work $d W$, then the conservation of energy is given by:

$$d Q = d U + d W$$

### Sign Convention

- **Heat gained by system:** $\Delta Q > 0$
    
- **Work done by system:** $W > 0$
    
- **Increase in Internal energy of system:** $\Delta U > 0$
    

### Application to Thermodynamic Processes

1. **Isothermal Process** ($\Delta T = 0$)
    
    - Since $\Delta T = 0$, the change in internal energy $\Delta U = 0$ (for an ideal gas).
        
    - The First Law becomes: $d Q = d W$.
        
    - **Interpretation:** The whole amount of heat is utilized in doing work.
        
2. **Isochoric Process** ($\Delta V = 0$)
    
    - Since $\Delta V = 0$, the work done $\Delta W = 0$.
        
    - The First Law becomes: $d Q = d U$.
        
    - **Interpretation:** The whole amount of heat is stored as internal energy.
        
3. **Isobaric Process** ($\Delta P = 0$)
    
    - The First Law is: $d Q = d U + d W$.
        
    - $d W = P d V$ (Since $P$ is constant)
        
    - $W = \int P d V = P \int d V$
        
    - $W = P \Delta V$


## **Free Expansion**

If a gas expands such that no heat exchanged and work done by gas is 0, then it is called **free expansion**.

$$P = 0, \quad \Delta Q = 0, \quad \Delta W = 0, \quad \Delta U = 0, \quad \Delta T = 0$$
