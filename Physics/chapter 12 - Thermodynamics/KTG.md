# Kinetic Theory of Gases

## Assumptions while describing ideal gas

1. A gas contains large no. of molecules. All molecules are identical and volume of a single molecule is 0
    
2. All molecules keep moving in random direction continuously
    
3. These move in a straight line. They will collide with each other and wall of container. It is always elastic.
    
4. Path travelled by a molecule between 2 successive collisions is mean free path.
    
5. Motion governed by NLM
    
6. Effect of gravity on molecules will be 0.

# Pressure in a Gas
#Derivation 
$$F = -\frac{2MV_x}{t} = -\frac{MV_x^2}{d}$$
5
$$F_{wall} = \frac{M}{d}(V_{x1}^2 + V_{x2}^2 + \ldots + V_{xn}^2)$$

$$= \frac{Nm V_x^2}{d}$$

$$\overline{V^2} = V_x^2 + V_y^2 + V_z^2$$

Because motion is random

$$\text{hence } V_x^2 = V_y^2 = V_z^2$$

$$\overline{V^2} = 3V_x^2 \quad \text{or } V_x^2 = \frac{1}{3}\overline{V^2}$$

$$F_{wall} = \frac{Nm}{d}\frac{1}{3}\overline{V^2}$$

Now Pressure = $\frac{F}{A}$

$$P_{wa} = \frac{1}{3}\frac{Nm\overline{V^2}}{d^3}$$

$$P = \frac{1}{3}\frac{N}{V}m\overline{V^2} \quad \boxed{d^3 = V}$$

$$P = \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{N}{V} \times \frac{1}{2}m\overline{V^2} \quad \boxed{\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{3}}$$

$$P = \frac{2}{3}\frac{N}{V}\frac{1}{2}m\overline{V^2}$$

# From Gas Law

$$PV = nRT$$

from pressure eq$^n$

$$P = \frac{mV_{rms}^2}{3V^2}$$

$$PV = \frac{mV_{rms}^2}{3}$$

equate

$$nRT = \frac{mV_{rms}^2}{3} \quad \begin{cases} m = \text{mass of gas} \ n = \text{no. of moles} \ m = \text{molecular mass} \ n = \frac{m}{M} \end{cases}$$

$$V_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{3nRT}{m}}$$

$$V_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{3RT}{M}}$$

$$K = \frac{R}{N_A} \quad mN_A = M$$

$$V_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{3KT}{m}} = \sqrt{\frac{3RT}{m}} = \sqrt{\frac{3P}{\rho}}$$

# Mean Free Path of a Gas

Mean free path of a gas is given as $l = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}nd^2}$

$(n = \text{number density}, d = \text{diameter})$

# Maxwell's Law of Equipartition of Energy

Energy associated with each DoF for any gas is $\frac{1}{2}KT$ (If for 1 mole then $\frac{1}{2}RT$)

**Note:** For vibrational DoF, energy associated with molecule will be $KT$ ($\frac{1}{2}KE + \frac{1}{2}PE$)

# Internal Energy (U)

The internal energy of a system is the sum of kinetic and Potential Energies of the molecules of the system.

$U$ is a function of temperature and $V$

$$U = \frac{nFKT}{2} \quad \text{(No. of molecules)}$$For 1 mole gas

$$U = \frac{FRT}{2} \quad (N_a K = R)$$

For $n$ moles gas

$$U = \frac{nFRT}{2} \quad \text{(No. of moles of molecules)}$$
