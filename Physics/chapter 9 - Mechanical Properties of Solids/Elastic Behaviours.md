
## Elastic Fatigue

Due to repeated strain on material, it loses its strength. This is why bridges and other constructions are declared unsafe after long time of use.

$\text{strain} = \frac{l}{L}$

$\text{stress} = \frac{F}{A}$

$F = \frac{AYl}{L}$

Now find work to relate P.E.

$\int dw = \int F dl$

$\int dw = \int \frac{AYl}{L} dl$

$w = \frac{AY(\Delta L)^2}{L \cdot 2}$

work done by external force is equal change in potential energy

$w_{ex} = \Delta U$

$w_{ex} = U - 0$

$U = \frac{AY(\Delta L)^2}{2L}$

# Forms of Elastic Potential Energy

1. $U = \frac{xY(\Delta L)^2}{2L} \Rightarrow \frac{AYL}{2} \left(\frac{\Delta L}{L}\right)^2 = \frac{VY}{2} (\text{Strain})^2$
    
2. $\frac{1}{2} VY\left(\frac{\text{stress}}{Y}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{2} \frac{V(\text{stress})^2}{Y}$
    
3. $\frac{1}{2} \frac{VF^2}{YA^2} = \frac{LF^2}{2AY}$
    

**Energy Density per unit Volume**

$$\frac{U}{V} = \frac{1}{2} Y \times (\text{strain})^2$$

## Torsion constant

When we try to rotate a rod about its axis while keeping one end of the rod fixed, then the torque required to rotate the rod by angle $\theta$ is $\tau = c\theta$

$c = \frac{\pi \eta r^4}{2l}$ (Torsion constant)

$P.E = \frac{1}{2}c\theta^2$

# Thermal Stress

When we increase temperature of any material, it expands. If a rod is fixed with rigid support at both ends, and temperature is increased, then the rod will not expand but a stress will be generated in the rod.

$\text{strain} = \frac{\Delta l}{l} = \alpha \Delta T$
$\alpha$ is a constant
$\Delta T$ is change in temp

$\text{stress} = Y \cdot \text{strain}$

$\text{stress} = Y \alpha \Delta T$

$F = AY\alpha\Delta T$

**Q)** If rod(l) is fixed by two support and coefficient of linear expansion(α) is prevented by rod and support i.e., find decrease in temp when it falls.

**A)** $N = F = AY\alpha\Delta T$

$F_f = \mu AY\alpha\Delta T$

If rod need to fall

$2F_f = mg$

$2\mu AY\alpha\Delta T = mg$

$\Delta T = \frac{mg}{2\mu AY\alpha}$
