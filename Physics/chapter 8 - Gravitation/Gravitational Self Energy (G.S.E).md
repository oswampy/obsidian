
G.S.E for a system of particles is work required to be done by external agent to assemble the system from infinity

$E_{self} = U$ (for a 2 particle system)

$E_s = W_{ex} = \Delta u = U_f - U_i = -\frac{G M_1 M_2}{r} = 0$

Note: for many particles

$E_s = -\frac{G}{2} \left(\sum_{i=1}^{n} \sum_{j=1, j \neq i}^{n} \frac{m_i m_j}{r_{ij}}\right)$

## G.S.E for a uniform solid sphere

Bring a layer from infinity to r, keep repeating by adding layer to surface of sphere

$S = \frac{M}{\frac{4}{3}\pi R^3}$

$M' = \frac{4}{3} \pi r^3 \times \frac{M}{\frac{4}{3} \pi R^3}$

$M' = M \left(\frac{r}{R}\right)^3$

$dm = 4\pi r^3 dr S$ (surface area $= 4\pi r^2$)

$dw = -\frac{G m' dm}{r} = -\frac{G M r^3}{R^3} \times \frac{4\pi r^2 dr M \times 3}{4\pi R^3}$

$dw = -\frac{3G M^2}{R^6} \times r^4 dr$

Integrate from min r (r=0) to max (r=R)

$dw = -\frac{3G M^2}{R^6} \int_0^R r^4 dr$

$W = -\frac{3G M^2}{5R}$

# Escape Velocity

The speed with which a particle is thrown from a planet such that it will go out of the gravitational field of the planet is called escape velocity of that planet.

To escape planet, T.E to be ≥0

$K.E + P.E ≥ 0$

$\frac{1}{2} Mv^2 + \left(-\frac{GMm_e }{R_e}\right) ≥ 0$

$V_{esc}^2 = \sqrt{\frac{2GM_e h}{R_e}}$

In terms of $g = \frac{GM_e}{R^2}$

$V = \sqrt{2gR}$
