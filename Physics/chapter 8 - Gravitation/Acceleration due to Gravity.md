

# Acceleration due to gravity at height h

$F_{h} = \frac{GMm_e}{(R_e + h)^2}$

$a_g = g = \frac{F}{m}$

$g_{gh} = \frac{GM}{(R_e+h)^2}$

at surface of earth (h=0)

$g = \frac{GM}{R^2}$

$g_h = \frac{GM R^2}{(R_e+h)^2 R^2}$

$g_h = g\left(\frac{R}{R_e+h}\right)$

$$g_h = g\left(1 - \frac{2h}{R}\right) $$
(approx)

# Acceleration due to gravity at depth d
$M' = \text{volume} \times \text{density} = \frac{4}{3}\pi (R_e - d)^3 \rho$  ...(i)

According to Newton's law of gravitation, the force of attraction on the body $P$ is  
$\dfrac{G M' m}{(R_e - d)^2} = G \cdot \dfrac{4}{3}\pi (R_e - d)^3 \rho \cdot \dfrac{m}{(R_e - d)^2}$  [Using equation (i)]

$= \dfrac{4}{3}\pi G (R_e - d) \rho m$

This force must be equal to the weight of the body $mg'$, where $g'$ is the acceleration due to gravity at a depth $d$ below the surface of the Earth. Thus,  
$m g' = \dfrac{4}{3}\pi G (R_e - d) \rho m$  ...(ii)

Similarly, if a body is at the surface of the Earth ($d = 0$) where acceleration due to gravity is $g$, then  
$m g = \dfrac{4}{3}\pi G R_e \rho m$ ...(iii)

Dividing equation (ii) by (iii), we have  
$\dfrac{g'}{g} = \dfrac{R_e - d}{R_e}$

$$\Rightarrow g' = g \left(1 - \dfrac{d}{R_e}\right)$$
