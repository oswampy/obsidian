**Gravitation**
- The force of attraction between two masses is called gravitation

**Kepler's observation (1)** After studying planetary motion he formulated:
#Law 
1. All planets move in elliptical orbit with sun at one of its focii
2. The radius vector from the sun to the planet sweeps equal area in equal time interval
3. The square of time period of a planet is proportional to the cube of semi-major axis of the ellipse. T² ∝ a³

**Inverse Square Law**
- The force of attraction b/w two masses is inversely proportional to the square of distance b/w centers. F ∝ 1/r²
**Mass-Attraction Relation**
- Gravitational force of attraction will be directly proportional product of their masses

**Combining Laws** F ∝ (m₁m₂)/r²

$$F = \frac{G(m₁m₂)}{r^2}$$

---

### **Gravitational Field**

The region around a mass in which gravitational force of that mass can be felt by another mass. Gravitational Field of any mass extends upto infinity

**Field Intensity (E)**

- The gravitational force on a unit mass at a point due to a particular mass at a point.

$E = \frac{F}{m}$

- For a unit mass -
 E = Gm/r² ⇒ $\frac{GM}{r²}$

---

## **Properties of Gravitational field**

1. For a point mass, G.F extends upto infinity and it dir'n is toward mass.
2. E is represented by field lines. Denser the field lines, stronger the field intensity
3. Two field lines will never intersect
4. If we keep a mass in gravitational field it will move along field line.
