# 3) Law of time periods

$F_c = Fg$

$\frac{mv_0^2}{R} = \frac{GMm}{R^2}$

$v_0 = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{R}}$ (orbital velocity)

R is dist. of particle from centre of earth

**Time Taken for one revolution**

$T = \frac{2\pi R}{v}$ (circular path)

$T = \frac{2\pi R}{\sqrt{\frac{GM}{R}}}$

$T^2 = \frac{4\pi^2 R^2 \times R}{GM_e}$

$T^2 = \frac{4\pi^2 R^3}{GM_e}$

$T^2 \propto R^3$

When any object is revolving in a circular path around a planet, square of its time period will be directly proportional to cube of radius of that path. In case of eliptical orbit $T^2 \propto a^3$ (i.e where semi major axis is 2a)
