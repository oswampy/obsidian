## Q) How much heat energy is required to convert 100g water at 80°C to steam at 110°C?

```
T = 80°          T = 100°C         T = 100°
  (100g)    →      (100g)      →     (100g)
  H₂O(ℓ)          H₂O(ℓ)            H₂O(g)
                                      ΔQ₃
```

Q = mcΔT

Q = L·m

ΔQ₁ = 100 × 4.2 × 20 = 8400 J

ΔQ₂ = 100 × 2260 = 226000 J

ΔQ₃ = 100 × 2.01 × 10 = 201 J

**Total heat = 236.4 kJ**

## Law of Mixture

If two or more substance at different temperature is mixed in a separate insulated container, the substance at higher temp will lose energy and substance at lower temperature will absorb energy until they reach common temperature (Tc).

Note: they have to be in same state, if not then find amt of energy required to convert one to another and see if its enough

Since container is insulated, ΔQ will be 0.

```
   T₁, M₁          T₂, M₂, C₂
      \              /
       \            /
        M₁+M₂, Tc
```

Q₁ + Q₂ = 0

Q₁ = m₁s₁(Tc - T₁)

Q₂ = m₂s₂(Tc - T₂)
 
 $Q_T$ = m₁s₁(Tc - T₁) + m₂s₂(Tc - T₂) = 0

### a) 10g H₂O at 10°C is mixed with 100g H₂O at 50°C, find final temp of mixture

10 × 4.2(Tc - 10) = -(100 × 4.2(Tc - 50))

42Tc - 42 = 420Tc - 21000

21000 - 42 = 348Tc

= 46.4°C
