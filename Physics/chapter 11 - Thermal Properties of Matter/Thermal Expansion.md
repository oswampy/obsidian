

When temperature of any substance is increased, its dimension increases for most types.

### Three types of expansion:

 1) Linear expansion:

Suppose we have a rod of length L at temperature T. If temp is increased by ΔT the rod will increase by dL



$$\frac{dL}{dt} \propto \alpha L$$


$$ ΔL = αLΔT$$

### Final length

$$\therefore L' = L + \alpha L$$

$$L' = L(1 + \alpha T)$$

α = coefficient of linear expansion

value of alpha depends on material not dimension

value - by depends on material
not dimension

2) Areal Expansion /Superficial expansion
   $\Delta A \propto A\Delta T$
   $\Delta A = \beta A\Delta T$    ($\beta$ = coefficient of areal expansion)
   $\frac{\Delta A}{\Delta T} = \beta A$
   
   $\frac{dA}{dt} \geq \beta A$    (Small change in temperature)
   
   $A' = A(1 + \beta\Delta T)$

3) Volumetric Expansion
   $\Delta V \propto V\Delta T$
   $\Delta V = \gamma V\Delta T$    ($\gamma$ = coeff. of volume expansion)
   $\frac{\Delta V}{\Delta T} = \gamma V$
        or $\Delta V = \gamma\Delta T$ (fractional change)
   $\frac{dV}{dt} = \gamma V$
   
   $V' = V(1 + \gamma\Delta T)$

---

## Relation between expansion

Initial       
$l = a_0$                              
$A = a_0^2$
$Vol = a_0^3$

Final
$l = a_0(1 + \alpha\Delta T)$
$A_{new} = a_0^2(1 + \beta\Delta T)$
$Vol = a_0^3(1 + \gamma\Delta T)$

$A = L^2$
$a_0^2(1 + \beta\Delta T) = a_0^2(1 + \alpha\Delta T)^2$
$1 + \beta\Delta T = 1 + (\alpha\Delta T)^2 + 2\alpha\Delta T$
If $\Delta T \ll 0$ i.e small, $(\Delta T(\alpha))^2 \rightarrow 0$
$\beta = 2\alpha$

$V = L^3$
$a_0^3(1 + \gamma\Delta T) = a_0^3(1 + \alpha\Delta T)^3$
$1 + \gamma\Delta T = 1 + (\alpha\Delta T)^3 + 3(\alpha\Delta T)^2 + 3\alpha\Delta T$
If $\Delta T$ is small $(\alpha\Delta T)^3 \rightarrow 0$
$\gamma = 3\alpha$
