-**Inertial Frame**
the frame whose acceleration is 0 whether moving with constant velocity or at rest is called inertial frame. $a=0$

-**Non Inertial Frame**
the frame whose acceleration is non zero is called non inertial frame.$a\ne 0$

- NLM is valid only for inertial frames, ex-ground
- To use NLM in non inertial frame(ex-lift,truck) we have to make some mathematical corrections which is called *pseudo force*
- Pseudo force has magnitude = object mass x acceleration, direction is opposite to frame of acceleration
	$F_{p}=-m_{obj}(a_{frame})$

## Pseudo Force
imagine a lift going up with acceleration a and has a block of m on it.
- this is a case of a non inertial frame.
Normal force is up and same as mg acting down. when we subtract the forces remaining force is just upward acceleration;
N-mg = ma
$N = mg+ma$

	- A is observing m from an inertial frame and B is observing from a non inertial frame
	- The **frictional force applied by the truck on the block is forward** —  
	because that’s the **only force making the block accelerate** to stay on the accelerating truck.
	- In case of observer B, the acceleration is 0 because relative motion between them is 0
Important note is that both inertial and non inertial frames have the **same value of N** that is N= mg+ma but in inertial frame there is **actually acceleration** but in non inertial frame, **pseudo force is required for the situation to work**
# Terms

**Mass**-  a fundamental property of matter that quantifies the amount of substance in an object.

**Weight**- Force with which earth attacts any object towards it is called weight of that object, SI unit-N. Weight = $mg$

**Apparent weight**- weight measured by any instrumennt. Apparent weight = $m(a+g)$

- Weight will remain same at surface of earth, but apparent weight will change depending on situation (lift,truck,etc)
