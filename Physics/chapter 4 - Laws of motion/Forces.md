# Normal Force
The normal reaction, also known as the normal force, is the support force exerted by a surface on an object in contact with it, acting perpendicular to the surface
- Normal force will act between any two surfaces in contac
- No. of normal forces will be equal to no of contact b/w surfaces
- Normal will always act from surface towards body
- Normal force will always be perpendicular to tangential surface at point of contact
- Normal force cannot pull anything it can only push
- Magnitude of normal $[0,\infty]$

# Tension
- Ideal String: no mass and no length (inextensible)
- Ideal Pulley: no mass and frictionless
- Tension is generated in a thear or string when someone tries to extend its length
- Tension at each point in an IS is equal but may be different in two different IS
- Tension will always be away from an object or point
- Tension can only pull

# Spring Force
- Ideal Spring: m =0
- When we apply a force on both ends of springto compress or stretch it, the spring will apply some force against the external force which is called spring force
- Spring force will be equal in magnitude and opposite in direction on both end of the spring
- Spring force is directly proportional to change in length of spring
	$f_{s} = -k\Delta x$ (-ve as its always in opp direction)
	$U_{s}=\frac{1}{2}(k\Delta x)^2$
	$\Delta x$ is change in length of spring
	S.I unit of K is $Nm^{-1}$
	K depends on material of spring ($\uparrow K$ for tougher materials)


# Frictional Force
Friction is a resisitve force which opposes relative motion between 2 surfaces in contact
- Magnitude of friction force will depend on nature of surface and normal force between the 2 surfaces
- Although friction helps us to walk on ground,kick a ball, etc. it also is responsible for **wear and tear** in many parts of machines
- To avoid friction among surfaces we can apply **lubricants** or also use **ball bearings/wheels**
- Magnitude of friction is given as: $$f_{f}=\mu N$$
($\mu$ is coefficient of friction, N is normal)
### Types of friction
1) **Kinetic friction:** kinetic friction will act between 2 surfaces in contact when they are in relative motion with respect to eachother
	- Magnitude is given by $$f_{k} = \mu_{k} N$$
	- Direction of kinetic friction will be opposite to the relative motion. It will be in opposite direction on both objects
		-dia
			![[kinetic_friction.excalidraw]]
	- Magnitude of kinetic friction is constant and independent of any external forces (*except Normal*)
2) **Static Friction:** It will act between 2 surfaces in contact if they are at rest with respect to eachother, and some external force is trying to move them relative to eachother, but fails in moving them
	- Static friction is a dynamic force whose magnitude as well as direction may change according to the external force applied
	- The direction of static friction force will be opposite to net external force applied
	- Magnitude of static friction has conditions as:
		i) if $f_{ex}\le \mu_{s}N$($\mu_{s}$ is coefficient of static friction)
			$|f_{s}|=|f_{ex}|\; or\;f_{s}=-f_{ex}$
		ii) if $f_{ex}> \mu_{s}N$
			No static friction as kinetic friction starts to act and the body is moving
