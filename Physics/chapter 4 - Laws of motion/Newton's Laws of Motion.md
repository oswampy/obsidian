
# First law

Every body continues to be in a state of rest or in motion in a straight line unless compelled to change by an external force
$$ \Sigma f_{ex}= 0 \implies a=0$$



# Second Law

The rate of change of momentum of a body is directly proportional to applied force and in the direction of it.
$\frac{dp}{dt}=kf_{ex}$ (experimentally k is found to be 1)

$$\frac{dp}{dt}=f_{ex}$$
#Derivation  of Impulse-momentum
$\int ^{p_{f}}_{p_{i}}dp = \int^{t_{2}}_{t_{1}}f_{ex}dt$
$\boxed{\Delta \vec{p} = \int^{t_{2}}_{t_{1}}\vec{f_{ex}}dt}$
- if $f_{ex}$ is constant
	$\Delta \vec{p} = \vec{}{f_{ex}}\Delta t$

#Derivation of F=ma
$\frac{d}{dt}(mv)=f_{ex}$                         (product of two quantities)
$m \frac{dv}{dt}+\vec{v} \frac{dm}{dt} = f_{ex}$
if $\frac{dm}{dt}=0$                                (mass is constant)
$\boxed{\vec{f_{ex}} = m \vec{a}}$


# Third Law
Every action has an equal and opposite reaction

- Force always exists in pairs
- Both forces of the pair act on different objects
