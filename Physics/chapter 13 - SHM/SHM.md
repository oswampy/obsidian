

# Simple Harmonic Motion

**Periodic motion:** When a body or particle is moving in such a way that it repeats its motion along a definite path after regular interval of time (time period).

• The path of periodic motion can be linear, circular, elliptical or any other curve.

**Oscillatory Motion** To-and-Fro type of motion: It need not be periodic motion or have fixed extreme position.

• Oscillatory motion where energy is conserved is periodic

**Restoring Force/Torque:** The force (torque directed towards eqm point acting in oscillatory motion is called restoring force or torque

**Damped oscillation:**

Oscillations in which energy is consumed due to some resistive force. Hence Total mechanical energy will decrease

**Simple Harmonic Motion**

If in an oscillatory motion, the restoring force or torque acting on body is directly proportional to displacement of body and is always directed towards eqm position, then motion is called SHM. It is simplest form of oscillatory motion.

---


**Linear SHM** When a particle undergoes to and fro motion about an equilibrium position along a straight line.

Extreme point is also called mean point and point of max displacement

**Angular SHM** When a body is free to rotate or oscillate about a given axis about a fixed curved path

The Necessary and Sufficient condition for SHM is F = -k x

ma = -k x

am d²x/dt² = -k x

d²x/dt² + (k/m)x = 0

Solution of above differential equation: x = A sin(wt + φ)

## **Characteristics of S.H.M**


x = A sin(wt + φ)

x = displacement distance of particle from mean pos. at a given instant

A = amplitude ~~Holds~~ It depends on energy of system

**Time period:** smallest time interval after which motion gets repeated

T = 2π/ω = 1/f

**Phase:** physical quantity which represent state of motion of particle x = Asin(wt + φ) ↑ phase

• Phase constant is initial phase at t=0: (AKA phase angle)


**Velocity of particle in SHM**

x = A sin(wt + φ)

a = -Aω² sin(wt + φ)

**Velocity & acceleration in SHM**

$x = A\sin(\omega t + \phi)$

$v(t) = A\omega \cos(\omega t + \phi)$

$a(t) = -A\omega^2 \sin(\omega t + \phi)$

max value of displacement will be $A$ since $\sin$ max at $1$
max v(t) is $A\omega$ 
max $a(t)$ is $-A\omega^2$

**Velocity as a function of x**

$v = A\omega \cos(\omega t + \phi)$

$v^2 = A^2\omega^2(1 - \sin^2(\omega t + \phi))$

$v^2 = A^2\omega^2 - A^2\omega^2\sin^2(\omega t + \phi)$

$v^2 = A^2\omega^2 - \omega^2x^2$

$$v = \omega\sqrt{A^2 - x^2}$$

max velocity when $x = 0$, $v = \omega A$

min velocity when $x = A$, $v = 0$

---

**Acceleration as a function of x**

$a = -A\omega^2 \sin(\omega t + \phi)$

$$a = -\omega^2 x$$

max $a$ when $x = A$, $a = -A\omega^2$

min $a$ when $x = 0$, $a = 0$

**Example:**

$x = 5\sin(\pi t + \frac{\pi}{2})$

$A = ?$ $T = ?$ $v_{max} = ?$ velocity at $t = 1$

$A = 5$

$\omega = \pi$

$T = \frac{2\pi}{\pi} = 2$

$v_{max} = A\pi \cos(\pi + \frac{\pi}{2}) = -\frac{5\pi}{2}$

**Velocity displacement curve in SHM**

$v = \omega\sqrt{A^2 - x^2}$

$v^2 = \omega^2 A^2 - \omega^2 x^2$

$v^2 + \omega^2 x^2 = \omega^2 A^2$

$\frac{v^2}{\omega^2 A^2} + \frac{x^2}{A^2} = 1$
