

**Compound Pendulum**

when mass cannot be considered point mass

[Diagram showing a compound pendulum with labels: d, mg]

Let MOI be I about hinged point

$\tau_{mg} = mgd\sin\theta$

$\tau = I\alpha$

$-mgd\sin\theta = \frac{d^2\theta}{dt^2}$

for small angle $\theta$, $\sin\theta \simeq \theta$

$\frac{d^2\theta}{dt^2} + \frac{mgd\theta}{I} = 0$

$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{mgd\theta}{I}}$

$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{I}{mgd\theta}}$

**Torsional Pendulum**

[Diagram showing torsional pendulum with angular displacement]

$\tau = -C\theta$

$I\alpha = -C\theta$

$\frac{d^2\theta}{dt^2} + \frac{C\theta}{I} = 0$

$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{C}{I}}$

$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{I}{C}}$

## Particle in Tunnel Through Earth

**Particle in tunnel through earth**

[Diagram showing Earth cross-section with tunnel and mass m at distance x from center]

$F = \frac{GmM'}{x^2}$

$M' = M_E \times \frac{x^3}{R^3}$



$F = \frac{Gm M_E x^3}{x^2 R^3}$

$F = \frac{GM M_E x}{R^3}$

$a = -\frac{GM_E x}{R^3}$

$a = -\omega^2 x$

$\frac{d^2x}{dt^2} + \frac{g}{R}x = 0$

$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{g}{R}}$

$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{Re}{g}}$

**Putting values** $T = 5000 \text{ sec or } 83 \text{ min}$
