
**SHM as Projection of Uniform Circular Motion**

Projection of uniform circular motion along a vertical path is SHM. The displacement of particle along x-axis will be displacement of SHM.

[Diagram showing a circle with radius $R$, point at angle $\alpha$, coordinate system with origin $O$, and point $(x, y)$]

$$x = R\cos\alpha$$$$y = R\sin\alpha$$ $$\alpha = \omega t$$ $$x = R\cos(\omega t), \quad y = R\sin(\omega t)$$
$$V_x = -R\omega\sin(\omega t)$$ $$a_x = -R\omega^2\cos(\omega t) = -R\omega^2 x$$

---

**Problem:**

a) A particle moves in circular path and starts at top at $t=0$

- $R = 4\text{ m}$, $V = 20\text{ m/s}$

i) eq. of $x$  
ii) find $T$  
iii) Time taken to displace by $30°$ on path  
b) fin $V_x$ and $a_x$  
v) find phase const in $\cos\alpha$

---

**Solution:**

a) In derivation we took $t=0$ at horizontal so it is displaced by $\frac{\pi}{2}$

$$x = 4\cos\left(\omega t + \frac{\pi}{2}\right)$$

ii) $\omega = \frac{V}{R} = \frac{20}{4} = 5$ rad/s, so $T = \frac{2\pi}{\omega} = \frac{2\pi}{5}$

iii) $\omega t = 0$ at $t = 0$, then $t = \frac{\pi}{6 \times 5} = \frac{\pi}{30}$

iv) $V = -20\sin\left(\frac{5t}{6} + \frac{\pi}{3}\right)$

$$a = -100\cos\left(\frac{5t}{6} + \frac{\pi}{3}\right)$$
