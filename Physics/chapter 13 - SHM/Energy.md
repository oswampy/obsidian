

In an undamped oscillation, two types of energy are involved: K.E and P.E.

K.E is due to motion of the particle and P.E is due to restoring force.

## I. P.E.

### i) P.E as a function of position

$$\frac{dU}{dx} = -F$$

$$\int_{u_0}^{u} du = -\int dx$$

$$\int_{u_0}^{u} du = k \int x dx$$

$$u - u_0 = \frac{kx^2}{2}$$

$u_0$ is P.E at mean position

If $u_0 = 0$

$$u = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$$

### ii) P.E as a function of time

$$u = \frac{1}{2}kA^2\sin^2(\omega t + \phi)$$

$a(t)$ will be max when $x = A$ or $\frac{\pi}{2}$ or $\frac{3\pi}{2}$, $Q = 0$

$$U_{max} = \frac{1}{2}kA^2$$

### II. K.E.

### i) K.E as a function of position

$$K.E = \frac{1}{2}m(\omega\sqrt{A^2 - x^2})^2$$

$$K.E = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2(A^2 - x^2)$$

$$K.E_{max} = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2A^2$$

### ii) K.E as a function of time

$$x = A\sin(\omega t + \phi)$$

$$\dot{x} K.E = \frac{1}{2}m\omega A^2\cos^2(\omega t + \phi)$$

$$\langle K.E \rangle = \int_0^T \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2(A^2 - x^2)dx = \frac{1}{3}kA^2$$

$$\langle P.E \rangle = \int_0^T \frac{1}{2}kx^2 dx = \frac{1}{6}kA^2$$

$$T.E = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2(A^2 - x^2) + \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2x^2 + U_0$$

$$T.E = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2A^2 + U_0$$

$$\langle T.E \rangle = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2A^2 + U_0$$

---

Total Energy over time will remain constant over time and position.

Kinetic and Potential energy both will change but sum will remain const.

### Q) Linear Harmonic oscillator of force constant

$k = 2 \times 10^6$, $A = 0.10^{-2}m$, $T.E = 160J$

$$k = 2 \times 10^6$$

$$K.E_{max} = \frac{1}{2}k \times A^2 = 100$$

$$P.E_{min} = 60$$

$$P.E = 20 + (x-2)^2$$, $T.E = 36$

i) SHM or not

ii) Mean position

iii) Max K.E.

**Solution:**

$$F = -\frac{dU}{dx}$$

$$\Rightarrow 20 + (x-2)^2$$

$$\Rightarrow 2(x-2)$$

$$F_x = 2x - 4$$

So it is SHM

To find mean position, force should be zero

$$2x - 4 = 0$$

$$x = 2m$$

$$K.E_{max} + P.E_{min} = T.E$$

$P.E_{min}$ at $x = 2$

$$K.E_{max} = 36 - 20 = 16J$$
