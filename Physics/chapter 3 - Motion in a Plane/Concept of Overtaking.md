#Q On a two lane road, a car A is travelling with speed of 36 kmh, 2 cars B anc C approach in opposite directions with 54 kmh each, At an instance when distance AB = AC both being 1km, B decides to overtake A before C does what is the minimum acceleration of car B required to avoid an accident

A)
A has V = 10 m/s, B and C has V =15 m/s each
$V_{BA} =5\hat{i}$
$V_{CA} = -25\hat{i}$ (opp direction)
$V_{C} = \frac{d}{t}$
$t = \frac{1000}{25}$
$t = 40s$
finding acceleration for car B to not crash
$s = ut+\frac{1}{2}at^2$
$1000=5(40) + \frac{1}{2}a(40^2)$
$a = 1m/s^2$

$1 ms^{-2}$ is the  acceleration when both cars crash so the minimum acceleration is above $1 ms^{-2}$
