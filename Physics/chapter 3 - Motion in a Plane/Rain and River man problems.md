#Q a man walks with velocity 4 m/s and rain falls with velocity of 3 m/s what is the velocity of the rain with respect to the man

A)
$V_{rm} = V_{r} +(-V_{m})$
$V_{rm} = -3\hat{j} -4\hat{i}$
this is velocity vector, now magnitude:
$|\vec{V_{rm}}|=5m/s$


#Q with what velocity should a man run so that the velocity of the rain is perpendicular to the man. Vrg = 20m/s, theta = 30

A)
$V_{rg} = 20m/s$
resolving velocity of rain into its horizontal and vertical components
$V_{y} = V_{rg}\cos30 \implies 10\sqrt{ 3 } \hat{j}$
$V_{x} = V_{rg}\sin 30 \implies 10\hat{i}$

$V_{rm} = V_{r} +(-Vm)$
$V_{rm} = (-10\hat{i}-10\sqrt{ 3 }\hat{j} -V_{m}\hat{i})$ (negative when it is falling)
$-10\hat{i}-V_{m}\hat{i} = 0$(for rain to fall vertically x component has to be 0)
$\boxed{V_{m} = -10m/s}$


# River Man problems

- Downstream - boat is moving in direction of river
	$\vec{V_{bg}} = V_{r} +V_{b}$

- Upstream-Boat is moving in opposite direction of river
	$\vec{V_{bg}} = V_{r} - V_{b}$
	
### case 1
-dia of different types of properties of these qs
	![[Riverman.excalidraw]]
	$\boxed{\vec{V_{BG}} = \vec{V_{BR}} + \vec{V_{RG}}}$
	${\vec{V_{BG}} = 4\hat{i} + 3\hat{j}}$
	we will take vertical velocity($4ms^{-1}$) of boat to measure time
	$t = \frac{16}{4} = 4s$
	to find point at which the boat arrives at other side we take horizontal velocity and multiply with time also known as drift.
	$BC = 3 *4s = 12m$
	to find the actual distance he had covered to reach the other side we use pythagorus
	$AC^2 = AB^{2}+ BC^2$
	$AC = 20m$

### case 3:
boat takes 2 hrs to travel 8km and back (total 16km) in still water. water has velocity of 4kmh.
$V_{BR} = 8\hat{j}$
$V_{RG} = 4\hat{i}$
one way is 8km and coming back is also 8
$V_{up}=8-4=4\hat{i}$
$V_{down}=8+4=12\hat{i}$
total time is distance travelled individually by respective velocity
$t = \frac{8}{V_{up}}+\frac{8}{V_{down}} = \frac{8}{3}hours$
$\boxed{t = 2hrs\;40mins}$

### case 4:
finding angle a person should swim so that he reaches a point directly across the river without any drift

a swimmer has velocity 20ms and the velocity of the river is 10ms
$V_{SG} = V_{SR} +V_{RG}$
for there not to be any drift, the horizontal velocity component of the swimmer should be equal and opposite to the velocity of the river(if the river is 8kmh right then swimmers horizontal should be 8km left)
$V_{RG}=20\sin \theta$
$\sin \theta = \frac{10}{20}$
$\theta =30^o$
