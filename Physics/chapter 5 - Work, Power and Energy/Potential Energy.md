Potential energy of a system of particles is the energy due to its configuration of its constituent particles.  
### Characteristics of P.E
- P.E is not defined for a single particle but a system of particles.  
- P.E is only defined for conservative forces.  
- P.E of a system of 2 particles separated by infinite distance is $0$.  
- - Absolute P.E does not have any meaning. P.E is always defined as change in Potential Energy.
- If initial point is not given in space we take it as infinity. On earth we take it as ground.
- For attractive forces, P.E is negative. For repulsive forces, P.E is positive 
		![[charges.excalidraw]]
		$U_r = -\frac{k q_1 q_2}{r}$ (i)
		$U_r = \frac{k q_1 q_2}{r}$ (ii)
- Whenever a conservative force does positive work, P.E decreases, vice versa.
- When external agent will do (positive) work, P.E will increase.
-Relationship b/w work done by conservative force & P.E  
$W_{\text{con}} = -\Delta U$
## Derivation of gravitational potential energy for a 2 particle system

Potential energy for particle systems separated by distance $r$ is defined as  work done by an external agent for bringing the particle from infinity to a distance $r$ slowly (so K.E does not change).

 #Derivation  of gpe of 2 particle system
	![[PE.excalidraw]]

(Here $f_{ex}$ is opposite to keep acceleration almost zero and balance the gravitational force of $m_1$.)
-but $f_g$ is variable as distance(r) is varied, so force has to be variable

$W_{ex} = \int_{\infty}^{r} F_{ex} , dr$

$W_g = \int_{\infty}^{r} F_g , dr$

Since K.E does not change, $W_{\text{net}} = 0$

$W_{ex} + W_g = 0$

$W_{ex} = -W_g$

$W_{ex} = -\int_{\infty}^{r} \frac{G m_1 m_2}{r^2} , dr$

$W_{ex} = -\left[ \frac{-G m_1 m_2}{r} \right]_{\infty}^{r}$

$W_{ex} = -\left[ \frac{-G m_1 m_2}{r} + \frac{G m_1 m_2}{\infty} \right]$

$W_{ex} = \frac{G m_1 m_2}{r}$

So P.E will be –ve of work done

$\boxed{U_{r} = -\frac{G m_1 m_2}{r}}$
