

When any particle, tied with rod or string, rotates in a vertical circle, the motion is called motion in a vertical circle.
-dia
	![[Verticle_circle.excalidraw]]

## **Motion with rod**  

A particle of mass $m$ is attached to a rod of length $L$ at one end and rotating in circle about other end. The rod can provide normal force, so at the top of the circle the minimum velocity required is $0$. Let us give a velocity $v$ to the particle at the lowest point. To complete the circle, particle should at least reach top of circle with minimum $v = 0$.
	Work done by tension is always $0$, so we can conserve M.E.

Using C.O.M.E between point A & B

$ME_A = ME_B$  
$KE_A + PE_A = KE_B + PE_B$  
$\tfrac{1}{2}mv^2 + 0 = 0 + mg \cdot 2L$  
$v_{\min A} = \sqrt{4gL}$   (Rod)

(We assume the zero potential at line A)  
(If at point B we need $v = 0$, so $v_{\min}$ is found as $\sqrt{4gL}$)

## **Motion with string**

When vertical motion is with string, string cannot provide upward force at the top of the circle. So we need some velocity of the particle at the top of circle.

No forces balancing gravity, so it acts as $f_c$.

$f_c = mg$  
$\dfrac{mv_{\min}^2}{R} = mg$  
$v_{\min B} = \sqrt{gR} = \sqrt{gL}$

Using C.O.M.E at point B:

$KE_{A} + PE_A = KE_B + PE_B$  
$\tfrac{1}{2}mv^2 + 0 = \tfrac{1}{2}m(v_{\min B})^2 + mg \cdot 2L$


$\tfrac{1}{2} m (v_{\min A})^2 = 2mgL + \tfrac{1}{2} mgL$

$\tfrac{1}{2} m (v_{\min A})^2 = \tfrac{5}{2} mgL$

$v_{\min A} = \sqrt{5gL}$ (string)

This is minimum velocity required at A so that object will not fall down at point B with string.

---

### *Q* A particle is rotating in a vertical circle with radius $2m$, $m = 5 \text{kg}$. It just completes the circle (string). Find $v$ at point C.

 $v_A = \sqrt{5gR} = 10$
$ME_A = ME_C$
$\tfrac{1}{2} m v^2 + 0 = \tfrac{1}{2} m v_C^2 + mgR$
$v^2 = v_C^2 + 20$
$50 = v_C^2 + 40$
$v_C = \sqrt{60}$
