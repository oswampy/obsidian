
• Work energy theorem says that total work done on any particle by all external forces is equal to change in its kinetic energy.  

$$W_{\text{net}} = \Delta KE$$  
#Derivation:  
Suppose any particle of mass $m$ is moving with $v_i$.  
Some external force $(F)$ is acted for some time and changes velocity to $v_f$.  

From 3rd equation of motion:  
$v_f^2 - v_i^2 = 2a \Delta s$  
$v_f^2 - v_i^2 = 2\frac{F}{m} \Delta s$  
$m(v_f^2 - v_i^2) = F \Delta s$  
$\frac{mv_f^2}{2} - \frac{mv_i^2}{2} = W_{\text{net}}$  
$KE_f - KE_i = W_{\text{net}}$  
$W_{\text{net}} = \Delta KE$
