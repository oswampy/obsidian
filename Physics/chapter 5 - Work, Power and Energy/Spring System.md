**Spring System**

$F_s = -k \Delta x$  
($k$ = spring constant, $\Delta x$ is displacement from original length)

Spring potential energy is the energy stored in the compressed or elongated spring.

#Derivation of spring potential energy
-derivation
	![[Spring_system.excalidraw]]
$F_s = -k x$

$W_s = \int_{0}^{\Delta x} F_s  dx$

$W_s = \int_{0}^{\Delta x} (-k x)  dx$

$W_s = \left[ -\dfrac{k x^2}{2} \right]_{0}^{\Delta x}$

$W_s = -\dfrac{k (\Delta x)^2}{2}$

We know $\Delta U = -W_s$.
$U_f - U_i = +\dfrac{k (\Delta x)^2}{2}$
If $U_i = 0$:  
$U_f = \dfrac{k (\Delta x)^2}{2}$

**While calculating spring P.E, we always take change in length $(\Delta x)$ from the original length.**

---

**Example:**  
a spring of length 1m is compressed to 80cm then 50cm
Find work done to compress from $80\ \text{cm}$ to $50\ \text{cm}$,  
$k = 1000\ \text{Nm}^{-1}$

Using WET:  
$\Delta K.E=0$
$W_{\text{ext}} + W_s = 0$
$W_{\text{ext}} = -W_s$
$W_{\text{ext}} = \Delta U_s = U_f - U_i$
$W_{\text{ext}} = \dfrac{1}{2}k (0.5)^2 - \dfrac{1}{2}k (0.2)^2$ ($\Delta x$ from 1m)
$W_{\text{ext}} = \dfrac{1}{2} \times 1000 \left[ 0.25 - 0.04 \right]$
$W_{\text{ext}} = 500 \times 0.21 = 105\ \text{J}$
