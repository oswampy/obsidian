### Work, Power and Energy

**Definition:**  
Work is said to be done on any particle by a force if the point of application of force gets displaced.

**SI unit:** Joules  
**CGS unit:** erg  
$1~\text{J} = 10^7~\text{erg}$

**Dimension:**  
$[ML^2T^{-2}]$

-conditions for zero work done:
- If F =0
- If $\Delta s$=0
- If $\cos\theta$=0, $\theta \in \frac{\pi}{2}, \frac{3\pi}{2}\dots \frac{(2n+1)\pi}{2}$
---

**Note:**  
Work done is a **frame-dependent** and **force-specific** quantity, i.e., from one frame, work done can be different for the same object by different forces.

**Mathematically:**  
$\mathrm{d}W = \vec{F} \cdot \mathrm{d}\vec{r}$

$$W = \int_{\vec{r}_1}^{\vec{r}_2} \vec{F} \cdot \mathrm{d}\vec{r}$$

**If** $\vec{F}$ **is constant:**  
$W = \vec{F} \cdot \Delta \vec{r}$

dot product:
$W = |\vec{F}| |\vec{r}| \cos\theta$

## Kinetic Energy  
• Energy associated with any particle due to its motion is called kinetic energy.  
$KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^{2}$  

• Work and energy both are interrelated. Energy is transferred from one form to another to do work and vice versa.  

Energy:  
• Energy is capacity to do work. Unit and dimension will be same as work.  

---
## Conservative Force:  
• If the work done by a force is independent of the path taken by the particle to move, then the force is called conservative.  
• Work done by a conservative force will only depend on final and initial position.  
• Every conservative force has potential energy. Energy will not be lost, instead it is stored in one form.  
Example: Gravitational force, Electrostatic force, Spring force  

## Non-conservative Force:  
• If work done by a force depends on path taken by the particle, then the force is called non-conservative.  
• No potential energy is associated with non-conservative force.  
• During work done, energy is lost in form of heat.  
Example: Friction,viscosity,etc

# Mechanical Energy
P.E and K.E are collectively called mechanical energy.
#Derivation 
$M.E = P.E + K.E$
$\Delta U=-W_{\text{con}}$
$\Delta KE = W_{all}$
$\Delta M.E = -W_{\text{con}} +  W_{\text{all}}$ 
If only conservative forces are doing work:  
$W_{\text{all}} = W_{\text{con}}$

$\Delta M.E = W_{\text{con}} - W_{\text{con}}$
$\Delta M.E = 0$

If only conservative forces do work, then the $M.E$ of the system will remain constant.
