
**1st Law**  
$\sum \vec{\tau}_{\text{ext}} = 0 \ \Rightarrow \ \alpha = 0$


**2nd Law**
#Derivation 
$\dfrac{d \vec{L}}{dt} = \dfrac{d (\vec{r} \times \vec{p})}{dt}$

$= \dfrac{d\vec{r}}{dt} \times \vec{p} + \dfrac{d\vec{p}}{dt} \times \vec{r}$

$\Rightarrow \ \vec{v} \times \vec{p} + \vec{F} \times \vec{r}$

$\Rightarrow 0 + \vec{\tau}$

$$\dfrac{d \vec{L}}{dt} = \vec{\tau}$$
$$\vec{L}=I\omega$$
$$\tau=I\alpha$$

## Conservation of Angular Momentum (COAM)

If net torque acting on a system of particles or any object is 0 about an axis of rotation, the angular momentum of that system will remain constant about that axis.
