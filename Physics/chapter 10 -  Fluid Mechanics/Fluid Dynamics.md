
## **Flow of Fluids**

 **Steady Flow/Streamlined/Laminar flow**  
If the velocity of the fluid particle at any point does not change with time

# Principle of Continuity

   This states that when an incompressible and non viscous fluid flows in a streamlined motion through a tube of non uniform cross-section then product of Area and velocity of flow remains constant at every point

    $A_1v_1 = A_2v_2$


# **Bernoulli's Equation**

• **Energy of a flowing fluid**

1. **Pressure Energy**  
    If a fluid is flowing in a tube of area $A$ and with pressure $P$. If in time $T$ it moves a distance $L$, work done by it is
    $W = PV$
    Pressure energy per unit volume
    $\frac{W_P}{V} = U_P = P$
    $$ U_P = P$$
     Pressure energy is always taken as pressure of the fluid at that point
2. **Potential Energy**  
    If a fluid is at height $h$ from ground
    
    $PE = mgh$
    P.E per unit volume
    $$u_{PE} = \rho gh$$
    
3. **Kinetic Energy**  
    If a fluid flowing with velocity $V$
    
    $K.E = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$
    K.E per unit Volume
    $$K.E = \frac{1}{2}\rho v^2$$


**T.E of a flowing fluid**

$K.E + P.E + \text{Pressure energy}$

**Bernoulli's equation states that total energy density of a flowing fluid** $(K.E + u_{PE} + u_P)$ **remains constant**

### **Magnus Effect**

The phenomena in which a spinning object curves through a fluid due to pressure difference created by its spin.

The pressure difference arises due to velocity of fluid in contact with the spinning object.

**Use of magnus effect:**

This effect used in sports like football, soccer, aviation, etc.
