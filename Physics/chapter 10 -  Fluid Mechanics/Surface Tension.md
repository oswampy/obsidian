## Types of forces

### 1) Adhesive Force

Force of attraction between molecules of different substance.

### 2) Cohesive Force

Force of attraction between molecules of same substance.

---

When a liquid is filled in container, molecules of liquid and container will attract each other with adhesive force. If cohesive force by water will dominate, the surface of liquid will become convex.

---

|$Hg$, glass|            |$H_2O$, glass|       |$H_2O$, $Ag$|

---

This phenomena in which there is a force on surface of liquid which makes a stretched membrane at the surface of the liquid is called surface tension.

Solids don't have surface tension so they are found in many shapes.

Surface tension $(T)$ force varies with contact length.

- $F_{ST} = Tl$
- where $F_{ST}$ is surface tension force
- $T$ is surface tension constant
- and $T = \frac{mg}{l}$
- $l$ is perimeter in contact

No surface tension - volume, only occurs at surface.

# Capillary Effect

A glass tube with small diameter is called capillary.

If we dip a capillary tube in water, pressure just below surface will be less than pressure above surface.

---

[Diagram showing capillary tube with labels:]

- Left side:$P_{0}$
- Right side: $h$, $P_0$, $P_1$

---

$P_1 = P_0 - \frac{2T}{R_c} + \rho g h$

$P = P_0$

$\frac{P}{1} - \frac{2T}{R_c} + \rho g h = P$

$$h = \frac{2T}{\rho g R_c}$$

---

Relation between radius of capillary $(r)$ and Radius of curvature of surface $(R_c)$

$R_c = \frac{r}{\cos\theta}$

So $h = \frac{2T \cos\theta}{\rho g r}$
