Fluid mechanics deals with behaviour of fluids at rest or motion.

A fluid is substance that deforms continuously under application of shear stress, no matter how small is the shear stress: $(h=0)$

### Density of fluid

$\rho = \frac{\text{mass of fluid}}{\text{volume of fluid}}$

$\rho = \frac{m}{V} \text{ kg/m}^3$

**Relative Density:** The ratio of density of a substance to water at $4°C$ is called relative density.

$R.D = \frac{\rho_s}{\rho_w}$

$R.D \in (0, \infty)$
