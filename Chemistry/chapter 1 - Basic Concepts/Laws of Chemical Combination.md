1) **Law of conservation of mass**
A+B->AB
sum of mass of reactants = sum of mass of products

#Q 1.5g of C2H6 on complete combustion gives 4.4g of CO2 and 2.7g of H20, Show o2 obeys law of conservation of mass

A)
$2C_{2}H_{6} +7O_{2} \to 4CO_{2}+6H_{2}0$
2 moles of ethane give 7 mole of O2
0.05 moles of ethane gives 0.175 moles of o2

- 1 mole gives 32g
- 0.174 mole gives 5.6g
$C_{2}H_{6}(1.5g) +7O_{2}(5.6g) \to 4CO_{2}(4.4g)+6H_{2}0(2.7g)$
both sides add up to 7.1g, O2 obeys law of conservation of mass

A chemical compound always contains same elements in the same fixed proportion by mass, regardless of source of method of preparation

4) Law of Reciprocal Proportion (3 elements)
When 2 elements combine seperately with fixed mass of 3rd element then the ratio of mass when they do so is a small whole no ratio to the ratio of the masses they themselves combine.

5) **Gay Lussacs Law**
When gases react, they do so in volume which bears simple whole number ratio to one another and to the volume of products formed.
