- Empirical formula is the simplest whole number ratio 
	the empirical formula of C2H6 is CH3

$$n=\frac{molecular\;mass}{empirical\;mass}$$
$$Molecular\;form{ula} = \;n*empirical\; formula$$

| Elements | %    | Atomic mass | Atomic Ratio | Simplest Ratio | Final Ratio |
| -------- | ---- | ----------- | ------------ | -------------- | ----------- |
| C        | 40   | 12          | 40/12=3.33   | 3.33/3.33=1    | 1           |
| H        | 6.7  | 1           | 6.7/1=6.7    | 6.7/3.33=2     | 2           |
| O        | 53.3 | 16          | 53.3/16=3.33 | 3.33/3.33=1    | 1           |
=>$CH_2O$ (empirical formula)
empirical mass = 30g (12+2+16)
Given molecular mass : 60 g
n = $\frac{60}{30}$
Molecular formula = $2*CH_{2}O$
=>$\boxed{C_{2}H_{4}O_{2}}$

- molecular formula is the exact number of different atoms in a compound
