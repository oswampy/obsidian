$$M = \frac{n_{solute}}{volume\; of\; solution (L)}$$
- Semi molar = $\frac{M}{2}$
- Bi-molar = $2M$
- Deci-molar = $\frac{M}{10}$
- centi-molar = $\frac{M}{100}$

#Q Find M if 120g of urea is dissolved in 1000g of water , density = 1.12g/ml

A)
Urea(CH₄N₂O) has molecular mass of 60
n(urea) = $\frac{120}{60}$ =>2 moles

$D = \frac{M}{V}$
total mass is 1000+120g of urea
$V = \frac{1120}{1.12}$=>1000ml or 1L
$M = \frac{2}{1} = \boxed{2M}$



# Molality

$$m = \frac{n_{solute}}{mass\; of\; solvent (kg)}$$

#Q Find m of 15% solution (w/w) of H2SO4 

A)
- 15g of H2SO4 in 100g of water(soution)
- mass of solvent = 100-15 = 85g of water alone
- no of moles of H2SO4 = 15/98 = 0.15
$m = \frac{0.15}{\frac{85}{1000}}$ (converting g to kg)
$\boxed{m = 1.7}$
