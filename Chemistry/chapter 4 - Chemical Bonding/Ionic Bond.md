# Properties of Ionic Compounds
- Their physical state is solid
- Melting and boiling points are very high as Electrostatic Force of attraction is strong
- Conductivity is bad in solid state as there are no free electrons
	in molten state conductivity increases rapidly(in water its increased by 81 times as the dielectric property/constant of water is 81)
- They show isomerism

## Energy Terms involved

1) Metal, $M \;(g)\to M^{+}+e$    (I.E/Energy absorbed)
2) Non Metal, $X (g) + e\to X^-$   (E.A/Energy released)
3) Ionic Bond, $M\;(g)+X\;(g)\to MX$ (lattice energy/Energy released)

# Born-Haber's Cycle
- To explain formation of Ionic Bond
- To calculate Enthalpy of formation and lattice energy ($\Delta U$)
(Side Note: sublimation is the whole process of solid to gas and back to solid, deposition is just a small part i.e gas to solid)
-**Born Habers Cycle for Nacl**
	![[BHC-NaClcalidraw]]
	enthalpy of formation($\Delta H_{f}$) is the sum total of all energies , 
	$\Delta H_{f}=\Delta H_{sub}+I.E(1)+\Delta H_{\frac{B.D.E}{2}}-E.A(1)$
	---
	Notes:
	- we need to convert all of them to gaseous so we can bond
	- energy is required to remove electrons from Na, so heat is supplied indicated by (+)I.E, **each subsequent I.E will also be +ve**
	- in the product side clearly one Cl atom is present so we convert the $Cl_{2}$ by making it half a mole, **in doing so the B.D.E required will also be half**
	- since in the singular chlorine atom, an electron is gained and so energy is absorbed indicated by the (-) E.A, **each subsequent E.A will be (+)ve**

-Born Haber's cycle for $MgBr_{2}$
	![[BHC-MgBr2.excalidraw]]
	Notes:
	- in the product side we can see the valency of Mg to be +2 so to convert it we must first sublimate it then remove the electrons twice indicated by the 2 
	- for bromine we can see its in liquid so we must vaporize it first
	- since in the product side there is $Br_{2}$ already it is a 1:1 mole ratio, hence the B.D.E will happen breaking the Br-Br bond(valency of -1 so we break it into 2) 
	- Once the bond is broken we are left with 2 Br atoms,**the E.A will occur separately for both of them** to form $2Br^-$, in calculation of the enthalpy of formation, value E.A will be multiplied by 2
