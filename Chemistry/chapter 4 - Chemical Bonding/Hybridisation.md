
### According to VBT (Valence Bond Theory):

For methane, $CH_4$:

$H - C - H$

Electronic configuration of carbon (atomic number $6$):  
$C : 1s^2 ; 2s^2 ; 2p^2$

Orbital representation (ground state):

$1s ; \uparrow\downarrow \quad 2s ; \uparrow\downarrow \quad 2p ; \uparrow ; \uparrow ; \Box$

-Excitation:

One electron from $2s$ is promoted to empty $2p$:

$1s ; \uparrow\downarrow \quad 2s ; \uparrow \quad 2p ; \uparrow ; \uparrow ; \uparrow$

So now there are $4$ unpaired electrons available for bonding.

---

### Bond Formation in $CH_4$:

$C(2s,2p) + 4H(1s^1) \Rightarrow 4\sigma -\text{bonds}$

But, experimentally all $C-H$ bonds in methane are equivalent (same bond energy and bond length).

---


## According to hybridisation:

Atomic orbitals of same or nearly same energy intermix to give new orbitals of exactly the same energy
For $CH_4$:

$2s + 2p_x + 2p_y + 2p_z \longrightarrow 4 - sp^3 \;\text{hybrid orbitals}$

These $4 ; sp^3$ orbitals form $4$ equivalent $\sigma$-bonds with $1s$ orbitals of hydrogen so all bonds are of same type

## Points
1. Intermixing of atomic orbitals of same or nearly same energy to give new orbital of exactly same energy
    
2. Half filled / full filled / empty orbitals can show hybridisation
    
3. No. of hybrid orbitals $=$ No. of intermixing orbitals
    
4. Hybrid orbitals are named after parent orbitals  
	 $1s + 1p \rightarrow sp$
	$1s + 2p \rightarrow sp^2$
	$1s + 3p \rightarrow sp^3$
5. Shape of hybrid orbital have a small lobe and one large lobe
6. The hybrid orbitals orientate such that repulsion should be minimum for maximum stability

# Geometry of Orbitals
1. $s + p = sp \quad (180^\circ,\ \text{Linear})$
    
2. $s + p + p = sp^2 \quad (120^\circ,\ \text{Trigonal Planar})$
    
3. $s + p + p + p = sp^3 \quad (109^\circ 28',\ \text{Tetrahedral})$
    
4. $s + p + p + p + d = sp^3d \quad (\text{Trigonal Bipyramidal})$
	- Equatorial bonds (between 3 Cl in same plane)
	- Axial bonds (above and below plane)
	- Equatorial–equatorial bond angle $= 120^\circ$
	- Axial-Axial bond angle is $180^\circ$
	- Axial–equatorial bond angle $= 90^\circ$
5. $s + p + p + p + d+d = sp^3d^2 \quad (90^\circ,\text{Octahedral})$

6. $s + p + p + p + d+d+d = sp^3d^3 \quad (\text{Pentagonal Bipyramidal})$
	- Equatorial bonds (between 3 Cl in same plane)
	- Axial bonds (above and below plane)
	- Equatorial–equatorial bond angle $= 72^\circ$
	- Axial-Axial bond angle is $180^\circ$
	- Axial–equatorial bond angle $= 90^\circ$

**How to Find Hybridisation (H):**

$H = \tfrac{1}{2} \big[ \text{valence e}^- \text{ of central atom} + \text{No. of surrounding monovalent atoms} + \text{negative charge} - \text{positive charge} \big]$

---

### **Examples:**


1. **$BF_3$**  
    $H = \tfrac{1}{2}(3 + 3 + 0 - 0) = 3 \Rightarrow sp^2$  
    Geometry: Trigonal planar  
    Bond angle $= 120^\circ$
2. **$BeCl_2$**  
    $H = \tfrac{1}{2}(2 + 2 + 0 - 0) = 2 \Rightarrow sp$  
    Geometry: Linear  
    Bond angle $= 180^\circ$
3. **$CO_2$**  
	$H = \tfrac{1}{2}(4 + 0 + 0 - 0) = 2$ 
	$\Rightarrow sp$  
	Geometry: linear  
	Bond angle $= 180^\circ$
4. **$ClO_4^-$**  
	$H = \tfrac{1}{2}(7 + 0 + 1 - 0) = 4 \Rightarrow sp^3$  
	Geometry: tetrahedral around O atoms arranged octahedrally around Cl  
	Bond angle $= 109.5^\circ$
