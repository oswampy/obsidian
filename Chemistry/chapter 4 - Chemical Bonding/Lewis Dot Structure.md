### LDS

- For showing valence $e^-$
    
- **Octet rule** → 8$e^-$ in outermost shell (Lewis theory)
    
- Octet can be neglected for **central atom** (e.g. expanded octet)

---

### Rules for selecting central atom:

1. Least number of atoms-> least electronegativity-> largest size-> $Z$ highest  
    (if there is a tie in any parameter continue down the chain to select)
    _(H and F can never be central atom)_

2. Octet of surrounding atom must be complete  
    _(H -> duplet)_

3. Central atom can have **8 or more $e^-$**
    
4. Central atom tries to be in its **maximum covalency**
    **Covalency values:**
    $O \rightarrow 2$  
    $S \rightarrow 6$  
    $N \rightarrow 4$  
    $P \rightarrow 5$  
    $Cl \rightarrow 7$  
    $C \rightarrow 4$  
    $Si \rightarrow 6$

5. If **positive charge is present** (e.g. $H_3O^+, NH_4^+$)
    it will be on the **central atom**
    
6. If **negative charge is there** (e.g. $NO_3^-, SO_4^{2-}$),  
    it will be on the **surrounding atom**

## Calculations for Lewis Dot Structure


i) $Q = \text{valence electrons of all atoms} + (\text{negative charge}) - (\text{positive charge})$

ii) $\text{Bond pair electrons} = 2 \times \text{No. of bonds}$

iii) $\text{Lone pair electrons} = Q - \text{Bond pair electrons}$
	Lone pairs (default) or  $=$ / $\equiv$ bond if required

---

**Examples**

1. $H_2$  
    $Q = 2,\quad \text{Bond pair electrons} = 2,\quad \text{Lone pair electrons} = 0$  
    LDS-> $H - H$
    
2. $O_2$  
    $Q = 6 \times 2 = 12e^-$  
    $\text{Bond pair electrons} = 2 \times 1 = 2e^-$  
    $\text{Lone pair electrons} = 12 - 2 = 10e^-$ (distributed around O)  
    We make a second bond to complete octet.(No charge)

3. $H_3O^+$  
    $Q = 6 + 3 - 1 = 8e^-$

## Formal Charge
 - formal charge is used in negative atoms when the covalency doesnt add up or the charge doesnt match
 - it is calculated seperately for individual atoms
	 $F.C=V.e^--L.Pe^--\frac{B.P.e^-}{2}$
 $F.C(D)=6-4- \frac{4}{2}=0$
 (D is the double bonded oxygen, we can observe the charge is 0)
 $F.C(S_{1},S_{2})=6-6-1=-1$
 ($S_{1},S_{2}$are the single bonded oxygens,they both carry a negative charge)
 $F.C(N)=5-0-4=1$
 (N is nitrogen atom,it carries a charge of +1)
 - we can see the overall charge added up is -1-1+1=-1 hence we get the atom as $\ce{NO3^-}$
**Note:**
- Single-bonded oxygen always carries $-1$ charge.
- Double-bonded oxygen is always neutral.
