### Valence Bond Theory 

1. Covalent bond is formed by overlapping of atomic orbitals (atoms should be close).
    
2. Only **half-filled** orbitals with electrons of **opposite spin** can overlap.
    
3. Overlapping must be less than $50\%$.
    
4. $\text{Extent of overlapping} \propto \text{Bond strength} \propto \text{Bond energy} \propto \dfrac{1}{\text{Bond length}}$
    
5. **Types of overlapping:**
    
    - (i) Axial overlapping  
        Results in $\sigma$ bond and always forms first.(strongest bond because of greater overlap)
        
    - (ii) Sidewise/lateral overlapping  
        Results in $\pi$ bond.

6. Overlapping orbitals must have **same sign** (not charge, just wave notation): 


---

## Axial Overlapping (Head-on Overlapping)

- **s–s Overlap:**
    - Example: $\ce{H_2}$
    - $1s \cdot 1s \rightarrow \sigma \text{ bond}$
    - **Always axial due to nondirectional symmetric shape of s orbital.**  
        **⇒ s can never form a $\pi$ bond.**

- **s–p Overlap:**
    
    - Example: $\ce{HF}$    
    - $1s$ of H overlaps with $2p$ of F
        
    - Bond: $\sigma$
        
    - $1s^1$ (H), $1s^2 2s^2 2p^5$ (F) → $1s \cdot 2p$ overlap (half-filled)

---
#### Example: $\ce{BF_3}$

- B: $1s^2 2s^2 2p^1$
    
- F: $1s^2 2s^2 2p^5$
    
- since $BF_{3}$ has 3 bonds with flourine->B needs 3 unpaired electrons so Excitation:  
    $1s^2 2s^1 2p^2$ (now 3 unpaired electrons)
    
- Overlaps:  
    $2s^1 \cdot 2p_x$, $2p_x \cdot 2p_y$, $2p_y \cdot 2p_z$

#### Bond Energy Comparison

**Q: Which has more bond energy?**
- $\ce{H-F}, \ce{H-Cl}, \ce{H-Br}, \ce{H-I}$

**A:**
- Less bond length → More bond energy
- $\ce{H-F}$: forms $1s$–$2p$    
- $2p$ is smaller than $3p$ (like in $\ce{H-Cl}$), hence stronger bond.

# Difference between $\sigma$ and $\pi$ bond

|                                                          **$\sigma$ Bond**                                                           |                                                       **$\pi$ Bond**                                                        |
| :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------: | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------: |
|                                                          Axial overlapping                                                           |                                                      Sidewise overlap                                                       |
|                                                  $s$-$s$, $s$-$p$, $p$-$p$ overlap                                                   |                                            $p$-$p$, $p$-$d$, and $d$-$d$ overlap                                            |
| Extent of overlap is more :<br>   i)Stronger bond  <br>   ii)Bond energy is more <br>  iii)Bond length is small <br>  iv)More stable | Extent of overlap is less : <br> i)Weaker bond <br> ii)Bond energy is less <br> iii)Bond length is long <br> iv)Less stable |
|                                        Only one $\sigma$ bond can be formed between two atoms                                        |                                    $\pi$ bond can be one of the two bonds between atoms                                     |
|                                                  $\sigma$ bond can be formed alone                                                   |                                       $\pi$ bond is only formed after a $\sigma$ bond                                       |
|                                              Rotation around $\sigma$ bond is possible                                               |                                      Rotation is restricted (due to sidewise overlap)                                       |
