_(Valence Shell Electron Pair Repulsion Theory)_

### **Points**

1. If lone pair is present → geometry and shape are **not** same.
    
2. If lone pair is absent → geometry and shape are **same**.
    
3. Electron pairs always repel each other and try to remain apart.
    
4. Different $e^-$ pairs have different repulsions:
	- $bp-bp$: least repulsion
    - $bp-lp$: mid repulsion
	- $lp-lp$: most repulsion
5. Geometry depends only on **H-value**.
    
6. Shape depends on surrounding atoms.
Note: Axial Bonds are longer than equatorial bond because of more repulsion at axial position.

### **Examples
$SO_2$** -------
$H=\frac{1}{2}(6+0)=3  (sp2)$  
No. of bonds = 2  
Lone pairs = $H - \text{bonds} = 3 - 2 = 1$
→ Shape = **Bent**
