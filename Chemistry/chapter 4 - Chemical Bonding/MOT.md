### **Linear Combination of Atomic Orbitals (LCAO)**

- Atomic orbitals combine to form Molecular Orbitals (M.O).
    
- Two types of interference possible:
	![[LCOA.excalidraw]]
	i) **Constructive Interference** ($\psi_1 + \psi_2$)
	- Results in **Bonding Molecular Orbital (BMO)**
	- More stability â†’ Lower energy
	
	ii) **Destructive Interference** ($\psi_1 - \psi_2$)
	- Results in **Antibonding Molecular Orbital** 
	- Less stability â†’ Higher energy
	- Represented by $*$
both can have nodal planes (N.P) which are regions of no electrons present

---

### **Conditions for Combination**

- Atomic orbitals must have:
    - Same or nearly same **energy**
    - Similar **shape**
- e.g., $1s-1s$, $2p-2p$

---

### Types of Molecular Orbitals

- $\sigma$ and $\pi$ orbitals form depending on overlap.
Atomic orbitals involved:  
$[\sigma1s, \sigma2s, \pi 2p_x, \pi 2p_y, \sigma 2p_z]$
Examples:

- $1s + 1s \to \sigma_{1s}$ (Bonding M.O)
    
- $1s - 1s \to \sigma^*_{1s}$ (Antibonding M.O)

---

### ** Example: $N_2$ molecule**

- Overlaps:
    
    - $2p_x + 2p_x$ (C.I.) â†’ $\pi_{2p_x}$
    - $2p_x - 2p_x$ (D.I.) â†’ $\pi^*_{2p_x}$
        
    - $2p_y + 2p_y$ (C.I.)â†’ $\pi_{2p_y}$
    - $2p_y - 2p_y$(D.I.) â†’ $\pi^*_{2p_y}$
        
    - $2p_z + 2p_z$ (C.I.)â†’ $\sigma_{2p_z}$
    - $2p_z - 2p_z$ (D.I.)â†’ $\sigma^*_{2p_z}$

### **Rules for MOT**

1. **Aufbau principle** â†’ Fill lower energy MOs first.
    
2. **Pauliâ€™s principle** â†’ No two electrons can have the same set of quantum numbers.
    
3. **Hundâ€™s rule** â†’ Degenerate orbitals filled singly before pairing.
    
4. **n + l rule** â†’ Works only for atomic orbitals, **not** for molecular orbitals.

## Bond Parameters

1. **Bond Order**

B.O.=$\frac{1}{2} (N_b - N_a)$
- $N_b =$ No. of bonding $e^-$
- $N_a =$ No. of antibonding $e^-$
- $\uparrow \text{B.O.} \implies \uparrow \text{stability}$

- In case of tie in B.O, molecule with lesser no. of antibonding $e^-$ is more stable.

- If $\text{B.O.} = 0$, molecule is hypothetical.

2) **Bond Length**
$BL \propto \frac{1}{B.D.E}$

### Q)

**a)Arrange in increasing order of stability**

Molecules: $H_2, \ H_2^+, \ H_2^{2+}, \ H_2^-$  

$H_2 : \ \sigma 1s^2$  
$B.O. = \tfrac{1}{2} (2 - 0) = 1$

$H_2^+ : \ \sigma 1s^1$  
$B.O. = \tfrac{1}{2} (1 - 0) = \tfrac{1}{2}$

$H_2^{2+} :$ Hypothetical  

$H_2^- : \ \sigma 1s^2 \ \sigma^* 1s^1$  
$B.O. = \tfrac{1}{2} (2 - 1) = \tfrac{1}{2}$  

Order: $\boxed{H_2^- <H_2^+  < H_2}$

b) Why $Be_2$ is hypothetical  ---

$Be_2 : \ \sigma 1s^2 \ \sigma^* 1s^2 \ \sigma 2s^2 \ \sigma^* 2s^2$  

$B.O. = \tfrac{1}{2} (4 - 4) = 0$
so it is hypothetical

Example: $N_2$  ----

Configuration: $\sigma 1s^2, \ \sigma^* 1s^2, \ \sigma 2s^2, \ \sigma^* 2s^2, \ \pi 2px^2 = \pi 2py^2, \ \sigma 2pz^2$  

$B.O. = \tfrac{1}{2} (10 - 4) = 3$  

(All orbitals hold max $2e^-$)


Example: $B_2$  ----

Configuration: $\sigma 1s^2, \ \sigma^* 1s^2, \ \sigma 2s^2, \ \sigma^* 2s^2, \ \pi 2px^1 = \pi 2py^1$  
(since both $\pi 2p$ orbitals are same energy they follow hunds rule)
$B.O. = \tfrac{1}{2} (6 - 4) = 1$
