# Purification of Organic Compounds (POC)

## i) Qualitative Analysis

→ Detection of element in organic compound

---

### a) Detection of Carbon and Hydrogen

$$\text{CO}_2 \xrightarrow{\text{Ca(OH)}_2} \underset{\text{Milky}}{\text{CaCO}_3} \xrightarrow[\text{excess}]{\text{Ca(OH)}_2} \underset{\text{Milky}}{\text{Ca(HCO}_3)_2}$$

**O.C** → CuO **C and H** → $\text{H}_2\text{O} \xrightarrow{\text{CuSO}_4} \text{CuSO}_4 \cdot 5\text{H}_2\text{O}$ (blue → white)

---

### b) Lassaigne's Method (detection of P, S, N, X)

$$\text{O.C} \xrightarrow{\text{Na/}\Delta} \text{Lassaigne's extract or sodium fusion extract }$$ **P, S, N, X** → (covalent) → (ionic)

$$\text{Na} + \text{N} + \text{C} \xrightarrow{\Delta} \text{NaCN}$$

$$\text{Na} + \text{S} \xrightarrow{\Delta} \text{Na}_2\text{S}$$

$$\text{Na} + \text{X} \xrightarrow{\Delta} \text{NaX}$$
$$\text{Na} + \text{P+O} \xrightarrow{\Delta} \text{Na}_3\text{PO}_4$$

---

### c) Detection of N

$$\text{NaCN} \xrightarrow{\text{FeSO}_4} \text{Na}_4[\text{Fe(CN)}_6] \cdot  + \text{Na}_2\text{SO}_4$$

$\text{Na}_4[\text{Fe(CN)}_6$ on reaction with Fe₂(SO₄)₃ gives:
$$\therefore \text{Fe}_4[\text{Fe(CN)}_6]_3 \text{ (Prussian Blue color)}$$

---

### d) Detection of S
$$\text{Na}_2\text{S} \xrightarrow{\text{CH}_3\text{COO}_2\text{Pb}} \text{PbS}↓$$ (Product is black)

**OR**

$$\text{Na}_2\text{S} \xrightarrow{\text{Na}_2[\text{Fe(CN)}_5\text{NO}]} \text{Na}_4[\text{Fe(CN)}_5\text{NOS}]$$ (product is violet)

---

### e) Detection if both N and S present in O.C

$$\text{Na} + \text{S} + \text{C} + \text{N} \xrightarrow{\Delta} \text{NaSCN}$$

↓

$$[\text{Fe(SCN)}_3] \text{ (Blood red color)}$$ **If both are present**

$$\text{NaSCN} \xrightarrow[\Delta]{\text{excess Na}} \text{NaCN} + \text{Na}_2\text{S}$$

---

### f) Detection of Halogen 'X'

$$\text{NaX} \xrightarrow{\text{AgNO}_3} \text{AgX} + \text{NaNO}_3$$

- AgCl → white
- AgBr → Pale yellow
- AgI → dark yellow 
- AgF is soluble and no precipitate will form

---

[Diagram showing reaction pathways]
***Precaution:***
**Before halogen detection to see if N and S are present with X:**

$$\text{NaCN} \xrightarrow{+\text{HNO}_3} \text{HCN}↑$$

$$\text{Na}_2\text{S} \xrightarrow{+\text{HNO}_3} \text{H}_2\text{S}↑$$

**If HNO₃ not used**

$$\text{NaCN} \xrightarrow{\text{AgNO}_3} \text{AgCN}↓ + \text{NaNO}_3$$ (white)

**If we test for chlorine without precaution** **Then we could mistaken AgCN for similar colored AgCl**

---

### g) Detection of Phosphorus

**Ammonium Molybdate** $$\text{Na}_3\text{PO}_4 \xrightarrow{(\text{NH}_4)_2\text{MoO}_4} (\text{NH}_4)_3\text{PO}_4 \cdot 12\text{MoO}_3$$ **Ammonium phosphomolybdate** (Canary Yellow)

## ii) Quantitative Analysis

### a) Estimation (% element) of C and H

↓C and H estimated by Liebig's Combustion Method

$$\text{O.C} \rightarrow \begin{cases} \text{CO}_2 \ \text{H}_2\text{O} \end{cases}$$

$$\%C = \frac{12}{44} \times \frac{W_1}{W} \times 100$$ $W_1$ = mass given mass of C

$$\%H = \frac{2}{18} \times \frac{W_2}{W} \times 100$$ $W_2$ = given mass of H

---

### b) Estimation of X (Carius Method)

$$\text{NaX} + \text{AgNO}_3 \longrightarrow \text{AgX} + \text{NaNO}_3$$

- AgCl
- AgBr → Ag
- AgI

$$\%Cl = \frac{35.5}{143.5} \times \frac{W_1}{W} \times 100$$

$$\%Br = \frac{80}{188} \times \frac{W_2}{W} \times 100$$

$$\%I = \frac{127}{235} \times \frac{W_3}{W} \times 100$$

---

### c) Estimation of S (Carius Method)

$$\text{O.C} \xrightarrow[]{\text{BaCl}_2} \text{BaSO}_4↓ + \text{H}_2\text{O}$$

$$\%S = \frac{32}{233} \times \frac{W_1}{W} \times 100$$

---

### d) Estimation of P (Carius Method)

$$\text{O.C} \xrightarrow[]{} (\text{NH}_4)_3\text{PO}_4 \cdot 12\text{MoO}_3$$

$$\%P = \frac{31}{1877} \times \frac{W_1}{W} \times 100$$

---

### Numericals

**a)** If 0.32g of O.C containing S produces 0.233g of BaSO₄, then % of S.

**A)** $$\frac{32}{233} \times \frac{233 \times 10^{-3}}{32 \times 10^{-2}} \times 100 = 10\%$$

### e) Estimation of Nitrogen

#### i) Dumas Method

• A weighing amount of O.C is heated with CuO in a atmosphere of CO₂

• C and H present in the compound are oxidized to CO₂ and H₂O while N₂ is set free

• Mixture of gases produced is collected over KOH (caustic potash) soln which absorbs CO₂

• N is collected in upper part of N meter

---

**Moist N₂**

$$C_xH_yN_z + (2x+\frac{y}{2})\text{CuO} \longrightarrow x\text{CO}_2 + \frac{y}{2}\text{H}_2\text{O} + \frac{z}{2}\text{N}_2 + (2x+\frac{y}{2})\text{Cu}$$

$$\frac{(P-P_1)V_1}{T_1} = \frac{P_2V_2}{T_2}$$

- P = Pressure of Moist N₂ at T₁
- V₁ = Vol of H₂O (Aq. Tension)
- T₁ = Temp in K
- P₁ = Pressure of Dry N₂ (Aq. Tension)
- At STP (P₂ = 1 atm or 760 mmHg, V₂ = Vol at STP, T₂ = 273K)
- 22.4L → 28g N₂
- 22400ml → 28gN₂

$$N_2 \text{ ml} \rightarrow \frac{28}{22400} \times V_2g,N_2$$

$$N = \frac{28}{22400} \times \frac{V_2}{W} \times 100$$

---

**a)** In Duma's Method 0.5g of O.C containing N₂ gave 112 ml

$$\frac{0.00125 \times 100}{1} \times \frac{112 \times 2}{1} = 28\%$$

---

**a)** In Duma's method for estimation of N, 0.3g of O.C collected gave 50ml of N collected at 300K and 715mm. Calc %N (Aq. Tension at 300K = 15mm)

**A)** $$\frac{(715-15)50}{300} = \frac{760 \times V_2}{273}$$

$$V_2 = 41.9 \text{ ml}$$

$$\%N = \frac{28}{22400} \times \frac{41.9}{0.3} \times 100 = 17.95\%$$

---

#### ii) Kjeldahl's Method

• In this Method N present in O.C is converted into NH₃

Kjeldahl's method is not applicable to compounds containing NO₂, NO, Azo group(-N=N-), Azoxy compounds (-N=N→-) and N present in (Pyridine, Quinoline) ring. Because N present in these compounds is not converted to NH₃ of (NH₄)₂SO₄) quantitatively

---

$$\text{O.C} \rightarrow \text{NH}_4\text{OH} \xrightarrow{\text{H}_2\text{SO}_4} (\text{NH}_4)_2\text{SO}_4 + \text{H}_2\text{O}$$

$$\%N = \frac{1.4 \times N \times V(\text{ml})}{W}$$

- N = Normality of H₂SO₄ (Molarity × 2)
- $V_{ml}$ = Volume of H₂SO₄ (1cm³ = 1ml)
- (1.4 is already considering ×100)
