

**Organic molecule (substrate)** $\xrightarrow{\text{Attacking Reagent}}$ Intermediate + Product  
Byproduct

→ Under the influence of an attacking reagent, a particular compound (substrate) may undergo either Homolysis (even distribution of $e^-$) or Heterolysis (uneven distribution of $e^-$) to form Intermediate species which is i) short lived and ii) changes to product immediately

### Types of Reaction Intermediate

### 1) Carbocations ($C^+$)

A group of atoms with a +ve charge on carbon atom having $6e^-$ in valence shell

## Attacking Reagents

|Electrophile|Nucleophile|
|---|---|
|→ $E^+$|→ $N\ddot{u}$|
|→ $e^-$ deficient|→ $e^-$ rich|
|→ attack $e^-$ rich sites|→ Attack on $e^-$ deficient site|
