

### Wöhler Synthesis

The reaction shows the formation of urea from ammonium cyanate.

$$\text{NH}_4\text{Cl} + \text{NaCNO} \longrightarrow \text{NH}_4\text{CNO} + \text{NaCl} \longrightarrow \text{NH}_2-\overset{\text{O}}{\underset{||}{C}}-\text{NH}_2$$

### A) Why does Carbon (C) have many bonds?

**A.**

1. **Tetravalency:** With a valency of **4**, Carbon can form **four bonds** (single, double, or triple) with other atoms, including other carbon atoms.
    
2. **Catenation:** This is the **self-linking property** of carbon atoms to form long chains (straight or branched), rings, and complex structures.

## 🔬 $2^{\circ}$ Prefix (Substituents/Side Chains)

The $2^{\circ}$ prefix denotes the substituents attached to the main carbon chain.

1. **R (Alkyl Group)**: $\text{C}_n\text{H}_{2n+1}$
    
    - $\text{CH}_4 \longrightarrow \text{CH}_3$ (**Methyl** / Met)
        
    - $\text{C}_2\text{H}_6 \longrightarrow \text{C}_2\text{H}_5$ (**Ethyl** / Et)
        
    - $\text{C}_3\text{H}_8 \longrightarrow \text{C}_3\text{H}_7$ (**Propyl**)
        
2. **X (Halo)**:
    
    - $\text{F}$ - **Fluoro**
        
    - $\text{Cl}$ - **Chloro**
        
    - $\text{Br}$ - **Bromo**
        
    - $\text{I}$ - **Iodo**
        
3. $\text{NO}_2$ - **Nitro**
    
4. $\text{Ph} / \text{C}_6\text{H}_5$ - **Phenyl**
    
5. **OR (Alkoxy)**:
    
    - $\text{O}-\text{Me} / \text{OCH}_3$ - **Methoxy**
        
    - $\text{O}-\text{Et} / \text{OC}_2\text{H}_5$ - **Ethoxy**
        
    - $\text{OC}_6\text{H}_5$ - **Phenoxy**
