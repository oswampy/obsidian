
## Solubility Product ($K_{sp}$)

$$AB(s) \rightleftharpoons AB(aq) \longrightarrow A^{2+}(aq) + B^{2-}(aq)$$

 for **sparingly soluble** elements

$$K = \frac{[A^+][A^-]}{[AB]}$$

$$K[AB] = [A^+][B^-]$$

$$K_{sp} = [A^+][B^-]$$ (saturated sol'n) (Temp dependent)

**Solubility** → Amt of solute present in 100g sol'n in a saturated state, at given temp.

Unit: g/L or moles/L

---

## Solubility and Solubility Product Relation

### i) Type 1:1 AB (AgCl, AgBr, PbSO₄, BaSO₄)

$$AB \xrightarrow{\text{electrolyte}} A^+ + B^-$$

$$K_{sp} = [A^+][B^-]$$
$$K_{sp} = (s)(s)$$
$$K_{sp} = s^2$$
---

### ii) Type 1:2 or 2:1 $AB_2$ or $A_2B$ (BaCl₂)

$$AB_2 \rightleftharpoons A^+ + 2B^-$$

$$K_{sp} = (s)(2s)^2$$

$$K_{sp} = 4s^3$$

---

### iii) Type 1:3 or 3:1 $AB_3$ or $A_3B$ (AlCl₃)

$$AB_3 \rightleftharpoons A^+ + 3B^-$$

$$K_{sp} = (s) \cdot (3s)^3$$

$$K_{sp} = 27s^4$$

---

### Q) $PbCl_2 \rightleftharpoons Pb^{2+} + 2Cl^-$

$$K_{sp} = s \cdot (2s)^2$$

$$K_{sp} = 4s^3$$

$$s = 0.279$$

$$\Rightarrow \text{4x solubility} = 0.279 \frac{1}{PbCl_2} = 279$$

$$Pb = 207.8, Cl = 35.5$$

$$\text{No. of moles} = \frac{0.279}{279} = 10^{-3}$$

$$4 \times (10^{-3})^3 = 4 \times 10^{-9}$$
