#concept 
$H_2O + H_2O \rightleftharpoons H_3O^+ + OH^-$
this is an endothermic process
$K_w = [H_3O^+][OH^-]$

Ionic product of water at 298 K

$K_w = 10^{-14} \text{ mol}^2 L^{-2}$ in $25°$

$K_w = [H^+][H_2O^+] = [OH^-]$

$K_w = [H_3O^+]^2 = 10^{-14}$

$[H_3O^+] = 1 \times 10^{-7} \text{ mol } L^{-1} = [OH^-]$

$\therefore pH = -\log_{10}[H^+] = -\log_{10} 10^{-7} = 7$

If $[H_3O^+] > 10^{-7}$, $pH < 7$

If $[OH^-] > 10^{-7}$, $pH > 7$
 
 Note: the value of Kw increases with increase in temperature
## Proving $pH + pOH = 14$
#Derivation 

$K_w = [H_3O^+][OH^-]$

$-\log K_w = -\log[H_3O^+] - \log[OH^-]$

$pK_w = pH + pOH$

$K_w = 10^{-14}$, $pK_w = 14$

$\therefore pH + pOH = 14$

(only valid at 298 K)

# Dissociation of weak Acid
#Derivation 
$HA + H_2O \rightleftharpoons H_3O^+ + A^-$

$t=0$ $\quad C \qquad\qquad 0 \qquad 0$

$t=t_{eq}$ $\quad C(1-\alpha) \qquad C\alpha \qquad C\alpha$

$K_a = \frac{[H_3O^+][A^-]}{[HA]} = \frac{C\alpha \times C\alpha}{C(1-\alpha)} \quad (\alpha << 1)$

$$K_a = C\alpha^2  $$

$K_a$ is dissociation constant of acid

**$$\alpha \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{C}}$$** — **Ostwald's dilution law**

#Derivation 
$K_a = C\alpha^2$

$[H_3O^+] = C\sqrt{\frac{K_a \times C}{C}} \Rightarrow \sqrt{K_a C}$

$pH = -\log[H_3O^+] = -\log\sqrt{K_a C}$

$pH = -\log\sqrt{K} - \log C$

$pH = \frac{1}{2}(-\log K_a - \log C)$

$pH = \frac{1}{2}(pK_a - \log C)$

$K_a$ is a measure of strength of an acid.

larger $K_a$, stronger base.
