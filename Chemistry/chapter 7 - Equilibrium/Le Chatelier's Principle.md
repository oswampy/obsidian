
→ can only be applied on reversible reaction (at eq^m state)

→ If we change conc^n, pressure, temperature, or add inert gas to a reversible reaction at eq^m, the eq^m will try to undo/nullify the change made by shifting in such a way direction as to undo the change

## **1) Change in conc^n/moles**
increasing reactants concentration shifts it forward and increasing product concentration shifts it to back

eg- N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃ [N₂] ↑, [N₂]↓ ↳ to undo effect it will shift forward

eg- 2SO₂ + O₂ ⇌ 2SO₃ 
Goal: max SO₃ production by : 
i) [SO₃] Removing or collecting → eq shift forward 
ii) [SO₂] / [O₂] ↑↑ → eq shift forward

---

## **2) Change in pressure**

Pressure on increasing pressure, eq shift to that direction which has less no. of molecules versa.

eg- N₂O₄(g) ⇌ 2NO₂(g) P↑, eq shift backward

eg- PCl₅ ⇌ PCl₃ + Cl₂ P↓ eq shift forward

eg- N₂ + O₂(g) ⇌ 2NO(g) P↑ shift ? Δng = 0 rxn so pressure independent

## **3) Addition of inert material**

→ At const. volume ↳ No effect

→ At constant pressure ↳ Eq shift which has more no. of moles

eg- PCl₅ ⇌ PCl₃ + Cl₂ He is added change at const vol → no effect const p → shift product


## **4) Change in Temperature**

→ On increasing temp. eq will shift to that direction where endo-thermic rxn occurs.

→ Reason- ↑T, heat addition eq disturbed/destroyed To undo effect, heat should be removed

eg- A + B + Δ ⇌ C + D T↑ eq shift forward as backward is exothermic

eg- H₂ + I₂ ⇌ 2HI + 3000 cal T↑ eq shift backward

**X5** eg- N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃ ΔH = -22.4 kcal/mol

λ) We need forward rxn so we will ↓ temp as now forward will be endothermic For pressure if we increase it will anyway sft forward so P↑ T↓
