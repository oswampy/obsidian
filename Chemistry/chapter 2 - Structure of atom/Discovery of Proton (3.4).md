# Anode Ray Experiment 

- Goldstein modified the discharge tube by using a perforated cathode
- On reducing the pressure he observed a new type of luminous ray passing through the holes or perforations of the cathode and moving in a direction opposite to the cathode rays
- These rays were named as positive rays or anode rays/canal rays
- Anode rays are not emitted from anode but from a space between anode and cathode
-Note: anode rays are the products formed when the electrons from the cathode rays are removed, when the negative charged is removed only a positively charged anode ray is left which heads back to the cathode

## Properties of Anode Rays (3.5)

- These rays deflect towards negative plate. It means these are made up of positively charged particles
- Property of anode rays depends on nature of gas hence they are **not fundamental** particles
- These rays travel in a straight line and have mechanical energy
- for different gases used in the discharge tube the charge to mass ratio of the positive particles constituting the positive rays is different
