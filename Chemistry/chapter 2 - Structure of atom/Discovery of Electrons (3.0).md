## Cathode Ray Experiment (3.1)
- William Crookes in 1879 studied the electrical discharge in partially evacuated tubes called as cathode ray discharge tube
- discharge tube is made of glass, about 60cm long containing two thin pieces of metals known as electrodes, sealed in it. Negative electrode is called the cathode and positive is the anode.
- When a gas enclosed at low pressure($10^-4$ atm) in discharge tube is subjected to a high voltage(~10,000V), invisible rays originating from the cathode and producing a greenish glow behind the perforated anode on the glass wall coated with phosphorescent material ZnS is observed

## Properties of Cathode Rays (3.2)

- Cathode rays travel in a straight line
- Cathode rays produce mechanical effect, as they can rotate the wheel that is placed in their path
- In the presence of electric or magnetic fields the cathode rays get deflected from their straight line path
- Cathode rays consist of negatively charged particles called electrons
- Cathode rays travel with high speed
- Cathode rays can cause fluorescence
- Cathode rays heat the object on which they fall due to transfer of kinetic energy to the object
- When cathode rays fall on heavy metals,x-rays are produce
- Cathode Rays posses ionizing power that is they ionize the gas through which they pass
- The cathode rays produce scintillation on the photographic plates
- They can penetrate through thin metallic sheets
- The characterisitcs of cathode rays(electrons) do not depend on material of electrodes and the nature of gas hence they are called fundamental particles
