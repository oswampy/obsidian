# Thomson's Model (4.1)

^9aed32

- Thomson was the first person to propose a detailed model of the atom
- Thomson proposed that all the positive charges and mass of the atoms are distributed throughout the volume of the atom and the negatively charged particles are embedded in them in such a number that the atom is overall neutral
- This was also called the plum pudding model
### Limitations
- This model was able to explain the overall neutrality of the atom, But it could not satifactorily explain the results of the Rutherford $\alpha$ scattering experiments though


# Rutherford's Model(4.2)
- Rutherford performed some scattering experiments in which he bombarded thin foils of metals like gold,silver with a beam of fast moving $\alpha$ particles
- The thin gold foil was malleable and ensured collisions was only by atoms and had a circular fluorescent zinc sulphide screen around it
- Whenever $\alpha$ particles struck the screen a tiny flash of light was produced
	Observations:
	1) Most(99%) of the $\alpha$ particles passes through without any deviation
	2) some of the $\alpha$ were deflected through a small angle
	3) Very few $\alpha$ particles were deflected by large angles and occasionaly $180^{ o }$
### Limitations
- This model could not explain the stability of atom, According to Maxwell's Classic theory, an accelerated charged particle liberates energy, So during revolution it must radiate energy and spiral into the nucleus
- It says nothing about the electronic structure of the atom, i.e how the electrons are distributed around the nucleus and what are the energies of the electrons
- It could not explain the line spectrum produced by these particles


## Overall Conclusions

- Atom spherical in shape have a size of about $10^{-10}m$
- Whole mass is concentrated in centre called nucleus having size of about $10^{-15}$
- Most of the space in the atom is empty as most of the $\alpha$ particles passed through the foil unaffected
- The positive charge has to be concentrated in a very small volume(nucleus) that repelled and deflected the positively charged $\alpha$ particles
- The electrons, equal in number to the net nuclear positive charge revolve around the nucleus
- The centrifugal force arising due to revolution of an electron balances the force of attraction of the nucleus and the electron
- the electrons are held close to the nucleus by the electrostatic force of attraction
