To explain the phenomenon of black body radiation and photoelectric effect, Max Planck proposed this theory:

## Postulates
- Electromagnetic radiations are packets of energies called photons. 
- The energy associated with these photons is directly proportional to the wavelengths of electromagnetic radiation.  $E=h\nu$
- Total amount of energy emitted or absorbed will be a whole number of quanta, i.e energy is quantised

# (i) Black Body Radiation
- It is a term used to describe the relation ship between an objects temperature and the wavelength of the electromagnetic radiation it emits
- When the surface of an object is irradiated with electromagnetic radiation, a part of the energy is generally reflected and some is absorbed
- The ideal body that emits and absorbs radiations of all frequencies uniformly is called a **black body** and the radiation emitted by it is called black body radiation

---

## (ii) Photoelectric Effect
The **photoelectric effect** is a phenomenon in which electrons are ejected from the surface of a metal when light is incident on it. These ejected electrons are called **photoelectrons**

the emission of photoelectrons and the kinetic energy of the ejected photoelectrons is dependent on the frequency of the light that is incident on the metal’s surface
$$hv=hv_{o}+\frac{1}{2}mv^2$$
## Minimum Condition for Photoelectric Effect:

#### Threshold Frequency (γth)

It is the minimum frequency of the incident light or radiation that will produce a photoelectric effect, i.e., the ejection of photoelectrons from a metal surface is known as the threshold frequency for the metal. It is constant for a specific metal but may be different for different metals.

**Note:** The number of electrons ejected does depend on the brightness of the light but the kinetic energy of the electrons does not

If γ = Frequency of the incident photon and γth= Threshold frequency, then,

- If γ < γTh, there will be no ejection of photoelectron and, therefore, no photoelectric effect.
- If γ = γTh, photoelectrons are just ejected from the metal surface; in this case, the kinetic energy of the electron is zero.
- If γ > γTh, then photoelectrons will come out of the surface, along with kinetic energy.

#### Threshold Wavelength (λth)

During the emission of electrons, a metal surface corresponding to the greatest wavelength to incident light is known as threshold wavelength.

λth = c/γth




#### Work Function or Threshold Energy (Φ)

The minimal energy of thermodynamic work that is needed to remove an electron from a conductor to a point in the vacuum immediately outside the surface of the conductor is known as work function/threshold energy.

Φ = hγth = hc/λth

The work function is the characteristic of a given metal. If E = energy of an incident photon, then

1. If E < Φ, no photoelectric effect will take place.
2. If E = Φ, just a photoelectric effect will take place, but the kinetic energy of ejected photoelectron will be zero
3. If E > Φ, the photoelectric effect will take place along with the possession of the kinetic energy by the ejected electron.

## Characteristics of the Photoelectric Effect

- The threshold frequency varies with the material, it is different for different materials.
- The photoelectric current is directly proportional to the light intensity.
- The kinetic energy of the photoelectrons is directly proportional to the light frequency.
- The stopping potential is directly proportional to the frequency, and the process is instantaneous.

## Factors Affecting the Photoelectric Effect

1. The intensity of incident radiation.
2. A potential difference between the metal plate and collector.
3. Frequency of incident radiation.

# Limitations of Planck's Quantum Theory
- It did not provide a theoretical framework or mathematical formalism to describe phenomena quantitatively. Planck introduced the concept of **quantization** empirically to explain blackbody radiation, but did not develop a full quantum theory.
- It only considered quantization of energy for electromagnetic radiation (photons). It did not address quantization of other physical quantities like momentum, **angular momentum** , etc. A more general quantum mechanics was needed.
- It did not address **dual wave-particle** nature of light/matter. Planck's theory treated radiation as consisting of discrete energy packets (photons), but did not incorporate its wave-like properties.
- It did not make any predictions about atomic/molecular structure or experimental consequences beyond blackbody radiation. A more developed quantum theory was needed to explain phenomena like **atomic spectra** .

# Emission Spectrum
When energy is absorbed by electrons of an atom, electrons move from lower energy levels to higher energy levels. These excited electrons **have to radiate energy to return to ground states** from the excited state, which is unstable. The emission spectrum is formed by the frequencies of this emitted light.
	**Continuous spectrum**: When the spectrum has no breaks or gaps between the wavelength range is known as a continuous spectrum. A rainbow is an example of a continuous spectrum.
	**Line spectrum**: When the spectrum has a discrete line that can be categorized as excited atoms is known as a line spectrum. Hydrogen produces a line spectrum.

# Absorption Spectrum
Absorption spectrum is constituted by the frequencies of light transmitted with dark bands when energy is absorbed by the electrons in the ground state to reach higher energy states.

The dark line pattern is seen exactly in the same place where coloured lines are seen in the emission spectrum.
