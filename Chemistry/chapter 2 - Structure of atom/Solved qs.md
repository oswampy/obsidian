### Q)ionisation enthalpy of hydrogen is 1312 kj mol find energy for n=1 to n=2

![](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAP///wAAACH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAICRAEAOw==)

$E_{n}=\frac{-R_{H}}{n^2}$
for ionisation
$\Delta E_{ion}=E_{\infty}-E_{1}=\frac{-R_{H}}{\infty^{2}}- \frac{-R_{H}}{1^2}$ (final - initial)
$1312=R_{H}$

now for n=1 to n=2
$\Delta E_{}=\frac{-R_{H}}{2^{2}}- \frac{-R_{H}}{1^2}$
$\Delta E=R_{H} \left( \frac{1}{1^{2}}-\frac{1}{2^2} \right)$
$\Delta E=1312* \frac{3}{4}$
$\Delta E = 984$ kj/mol

## Q) wavelength of 486 nm in balmer series correspond to which transition

$\frac{1}{\lambda}=Rz^2\left( \frac{1}{2^2}-\frac{1}{x^2} \right)$
$\frac{1}{x^2}=\frac{1}{2^2}-\frac{1}{R\lambda}$
$\frac{1}{x^2}=\frac{1}{2^2}-\frac{1}{R* 486*10^{-9}}$
we get x as approximately 4
so the transition is $4\to 2$



### Q)a 5g sample on combustion gives 8.8g CO2 and 5.4g H2O find empirical formula

We are given: mass of sample $= 5\ \text{g}$, mass of $\text{CO}_2 = 8.8\ \text{g}$, mass of $\text{H}_2\text{O} = 5.4\ \text{g}$.

Moles of $\text{CO}_2 = \tfrac{8.8}{44} = 0.2$, so moles of C $= 0.2$, mass of C $= 0.2 \times 12 = 2.4\ \text{g}$.

Moles of $\text{H}_2\text{O} = \tfrac{5.4}{18} = 0.3$, so moles of H $= 0.3 \times 2 = 0.6$, mass of H $= 0.6\ \text{g}$.

Mass of O $= 5 - (2.4 + 0.6) = 2.0\ \text{g}$, moles of O $= \tfrac{2.0}{16} = 0.125$.

Ratio: $C:H:O = \tfrac{0.2}{0.125} : \tfrac{0.6}{0.125} : \tfrac{0.125}{0.125} = 1.6 : 4.8 : 1$.

Multiply by $5 \implies 8:24:5$.

**Empirical formula = $C_8H_{24}O_5$**.
