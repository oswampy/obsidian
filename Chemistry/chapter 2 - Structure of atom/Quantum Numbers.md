quantum numbers came from Schrodinger's wave equation to explain the behavior of electrons and their orbitals.

Principle Q.N(n)
- this is basically just the shell number like K,L,M,N where K (n)= 1 L (n)= 2
- it gives the **size and energy** of each orbital

Azimuthal Q.N(l) ^356442
- to get the max l number we use l = n-1, ex we have L shell then the l goes from 0,1
- the values of each subshell are s=0,p=1,d=2,f=3
- it gives the **shape** of the orbitals:

Magnetic Q.N(m)
- m value depends on l value, suppose l is 3 then m will be from the negative to the positive value of 3 like -3,-2,-1,0,1,2,3
- it gives **orientation** AND number of orbitals


Spin Q.N(s)(not from Schrodinger's eq)
- an electron rotating about its own axis can have +1/2 or -1/2 spin which is clockwise or anticlockwise depending on what u consider. 
- In one orbital u can have a max of 2e and they HAVE to have opposite spin
