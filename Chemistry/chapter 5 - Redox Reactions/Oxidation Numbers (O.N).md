**Redox Reaction:** A reaction involves both oxidation and reduction
## Rules to find O.N (O.S):

#### General O.N's:

i) **Alkali Gr-1**  
$O.N \rightarrow +1$

ii) **Alkaline Earth Metals Gr-2**  
$O.N \rightarrow +2$

iii) **Boron Family Gr-13**  
$O.N \rightarrow +3$

iv) **Carbon Family Gr-14**  
$O.N \rightarrow +4$

v) **Nitrogen Family Gr-15**  
$O.N \rightarrow -3$

vi) **Oxygen Family Gr-16**  
$O.N \rightarrow -2$

vii) **Halogens Gr-17**  
$O.N \rightarrow -1$

---
## Additional Rules:

1. **O.N of an atom or molecule in native state is 0**
    
2. **O.N of a free, neutral element/molecule is 0 (monoatomic)**
    
3. **O.N of Ion is equal to its charge (Monoatomic ion)**
    
4. **Compounds of Metal and non-metal** we assign:
    - Metals with $+ve$ O.N
    - Non-metals with $-ve$ O.N
5. **Compounds of Non-metals (Both non-metals)**We assign 
	- More E.N with $-ve$ O.N
    - Less E.N with $+ve$ O.N
6. **O.N of Hydrogen**
    - $+1 \rightarrow$ Generally
    - $-1 \rightarrow$ Metals
7. **O.N of Oxygen**
    - $-2 \rightarrow$ Generally
    - $-1 \rightarrow$ Peroxides like $H_2O_2$
    - $+1 \rightarrow$ $O_2F_2$
    - $+2 \rightarrow$ $OF_2$
8. **Total O.N of atom an element in polyatomic ion will be sum of all charges on polyatomic ion**
