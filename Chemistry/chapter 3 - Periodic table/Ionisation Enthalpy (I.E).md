## Successive I.E for same atom
$M$-> $M^{+}+ e^{-} \implies H_{I(1)}$ 
$M^+$->$M^{++}+ e^{-} \implies H_{I(2)}$ 
$M^{++}$->$M^{+++}+ e^{-} \implies H_{I(3)}$ 

if it is same atom then $H_{I(3)}$ > $H_{I(2)}$ > $H_{I(1)}$

## Factors on which I.E depends

1) $I.E$ $\propto$ $\frac{1}{z}$

2) $I.E \propto Nuclear\; Charge$

3) Half and fully filled e config is stable => higher I.E 

4) Penetration effect
	$s>p>d>f$ (how long they can stay close to nucleus) applies to elements beyond lanthanum(z=57+)
	 
	 but in terms of stability, if there is a half filled d orbital and full filled p orbital d will have more I.E  as $s<p<d<$$\text{f}$ in term of stability 

In order of priority,the factors when comparing different elements goes 
(3)>(2)>(1)>(4)

### Variation along period

## #Q compare $\Delta H_{I(1)}$ and $\Delta H_{I(2)}$ for Cr And Mn
Cr- $4s^13d^5$
Mn- $4s^23d^5$
$Cr$-> $Cr^{+}+ e -> 4s^03d^5$  $\Delta H_{I(1)}$          ([[Periodic Table]])
$Mn$-> $Mn^+ + e -> 4s^13d^5$ $\Delta H_{I(1)}$

removing the 4s1 and leaving a stable 3d5 is easier than removing 1 from 4s2
since it requires less energy and the nuclear charge is lower in cr (Mn>Cr because of atomic number)
so Mn>Cr

$Cr^{+}$->$Cr^{++}+ e -> 4s^03d^4$ $\Delta H_{I(2)}$
$Mn^+$->$Mn^{++}+ e -> 4s^03d^5$ $\Delta H_{I(2)}$

removing the 1 from 3d5 would break the stability and require lots of energy whereas removing 1 from $4s^{1}$in Mn is easy as it leaves a stable 3d5
so Cr>Mn

-key concepts:
- when the e config becomes stable(half or full filled) then it will have lower I.E
- when taking away from a stable config it will require high energy 

## Variation down the group

generally the size increases and the nuclear charge and I.E decreases 

#Exception

expected: B>Al>Ga>In>Tl                    I.E decreasing ->

#concept 
This is because **Thallium** has poorest f shielding and undergoes lanthanoid contraction, so the nucleus grabs onto the electron easily and the size decreases. **Gallium** has poor d shielding and undergoes transition contraction and for the same reason its higher up but since d shielding is better than f its lower than thallium

### Metallic and Non-Metallic Characteristics (s and p block)
Down the group:size increases, I.E decreases, electron removal is easy => metallic characters **increase**, non metallic characters **decrease**

Across the period: size decreases, I.E increases, electron removal is hard => metallic characters **decrease**, non metallic characters **increase**
