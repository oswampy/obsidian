The reluctance of ns electrons in valence shell to participate in bonding as we go down the group

- ONLY observed in **p block** and that too mainly in **grp 13,14,15**

Reason for I.P.E:

Since the **last elements** of each group is after atomic number of lanthanoids (z = 57+) they will have f shells which mean they have **very poor shielding** and so the nucleus always take 2 electrons from them, so in group 13 if B has an O.N of +3 then the last element thallium (Tl) will have an O.N of 1 (3-2)

This is why $PbI_2$ is possible and $PbI_{4}$ is not, Pb is in grp 14(O.N = +4) and is the last element so the O.N (which is the most stable state) is +2

-> Top elements are more stable in $\uparrow$O.N
-> bottom elements are more stable in $\downarrow$O.N
