## 1) Ionic Radius
- when an electron is removed from the atom, the nuclear attraction increases on the remaining electrons so the size decreases
- when it loses e, it shrinks and when it gains it expands
- In descending ionic radius Anion > Neutral > Cation
- $N^{-}< N^{2-} < N^{3-}$
- in isoelectronic species only with ions : size $\propto \frac{1}{z}$ (not noble gases tho)
	$Ar<K^+<Ca^{2+}$
- ionic radii vary in:
	-inverse proportion to effective nuclear charge
	-direct proportion to screening effect
