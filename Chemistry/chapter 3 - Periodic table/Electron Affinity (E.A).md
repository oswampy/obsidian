- Tendency to add electrons in an isolated,neutral,gaseous atom
- Happens generally in non metals
- when an electron is added, the atom releases energy to become more stable and the E.A becomes negative

- Electron affinity is a general term and can be considered one of two ways, the thermodynamic way:electron gain enthalpy ($\Delta H$) or Electron Affinity ($E.A$)
		the only difference between the two is sign changes as $-\Delta H =  EA$
	the first electron affinity or gain enthalpy is always opposite of the subsequent ones

$N + e \to N^-$ ($E.A = +ve$, $\Delta H = -ve$)
$N^{-}+ e \to N^{2-}$ ($E.A = -ve$, $\Delta H = +ve$)
$N^{2-}+ e \to N^{3-}$ ($E.A = -ve$, $\Delta H = +ve$)

Each subsequent  E.A /del h is greater than the previous so the 2nd EA would be more negative than however much the first EA is positive and the 3rd EA is more negative than the 2nd one

## Factors on which E.A depends

1) Atomic Size **increases**, Nuclear charge decreases, E.A **decreases**

2) Electron configuration (half/full filled) -> I.E is more but E.A is less because they dont want to lose or gain electrons

3) E.A $\propto$ Nuclear Charge 

### Variation along period
L to R -> size **decreases** , nuclear charge increases, E.A **increases**,$\Delta H$decreases

### Variation down group
T to B-> size **increases**, nuclear charge decreases, E.A **decreases**, $\Delta H$ increases
