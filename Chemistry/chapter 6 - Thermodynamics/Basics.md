 
Thermodynamics is Heat in Motion
$\text{system} + \text{surrounding} = \text{universe}$

## Boundaries:

- Real
- Imaginary
- Flexible
- Rigid
- Diathermic (heat exchange can happen, conducting)
- Adiabatic (Heat exchange cannot happen, insulator)

## Types of Systems

### 1) Open System

- Mass exchange + energy exchange
- Heat work
- Real boundary & Conducting

### 2) Closed System

- No mass exchange, only energy exchange
- Real Boundary & Conducting

### 3) Isolated System (Theoretical)

- No mass exchange or energy exchange
- Adiabatic Real Boundary

## State Function

Property that depends only on state of a system (condition)

Change only depends on initial and final values, not the path.

**Ex:** Pressure, Volume, Temperature, Enthalpy, Entropy(S), Internal Energy (u), Gibbs free Energy (G), no. of moles

## Path Function

Property that depends on path or process followed to reach a state, not just initial and final conditions.

**Ex:** Heat $(q)$, work $(w)$

## Types of Thermodynamic Process:

### 1) Isothermal Process
- Constant temperature $(\Delta T = 0)$
- In ideal gas: if $\Delta T = 0$, $\Delta E = 0$, $E = KE \propto T$
- $T = \text{const}$, $P \propto \frac{1}{V}$ (Boyle's Law)
### 2) Adiabatic Process

$\Delta q = 0$
### 3) Isobaric Process

- Constant pressure, $\Delta p = 0$
### 4) Isochoric Process
$\Delta V = 0$

## Reversible vs Irreversible

| **Reversible**                           | **Irreversible**                            |
| ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| • Occurs in infinite small steps         | • Occurs at once                            |
| • Infinite time to finish                | • Finite time to finish                     |
| • Slow                                   | • Fast                                      |
| • Each step is in eqm with previous step | • Eqm only in initial and final, not in b/w |
| In Piston container case:                | In Piston Container case                    |
| $P_{ext}= P_{in} \pm dP$                 | $P_{ext}= P_{in} \pm \Delta P$              |
|                                          | external pressure is constant               |
## Heat Capacity

**q ∝ ΔT** or **q = CΔT**

Magnitude of coefficient depends on size, composition, nature of system.

**C = q/ΔT**

Unit is Joules/Kelvin (JK⁻¹) or J/C
