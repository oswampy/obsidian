Form of transfer of energy when there is temperature difference
- Unit: Joule
- Common unit: calorie
- $1 \text{ calorie} = 4.1823 \text{ J}$

## **Specific heat capacity:** $$C = \frac{Q}{m \Delta T}$$

$$C = 4.2 \text{ J/g°C to raise temp of 1g of water by 1°C}$$


Amount of energy required in specific heat capacity of water $(4.23 \text{ for water})$

$$C_V = \text{sp. heat capacity at const volume, } Q = C_V$$ $$C_P = \text{sp. heat capacity at const pressure, } Q = C_P$$
Process types:

- $C_{\text{Isothermal}} \rightarrow \infty$
- $C_{\text{Adiabatic}} = 0$

If we use $n$ moles instead of mass, it becomes molar specific heat capacity.

Amount of energy required to raise temp of gas by 1°C:

$$C_P = \frac{Q}{n \Delta T}, \quad C_V = \frac{Q}{n \Delta T}$$

**Example: $O_2$ gas**

- $c_V = x$ when we use 1g to be raised by 1°C
- $C_V = ?$ when we use 1 mole to be raised by 1°C
- So if $c_V = a$, then $C_V = 32a$ \[for $O_2$]

$$C_P - C_V = R \text{ for any gas}$$ $$C_V = Mc_v$$ $$C_P = Mc_P$$

## Degree of Freedom

$f = \text{degree of freedom (depends on atomicity)}$

$$C_V = \frac{f}{2}R$$ $$\therefore C_P = \left(\frac{f}{2} + 1\right)R$$

### **$f$ values:**

- Monoatomic gas: $f = 3$ $(He, Ne, Ar)$
- Diatomic gas: $f = 5$ $(O_2, H_2, N_2)$
- Polyatomic gas: $f = 6$ $(SO_2, O_3)$

$$\frac{C_P}{C_V} = \gamma$$

**For monoatomic gas:** $$f = 3, \quad C_V = \frac{3}{2}R, \quad C_P = \frac{5}{2}R, \quad \gamma = \frac{5}{3}R = 1.67$$

**For diatomic gas:** $$f = 5, \quad C_V = \frac{5}{2}R, \quad C_P = \frac{7}{2}R, \quad \gamma = \frac{7}{5}R = 1.4$$

**For polyatomic gas:** $$f = 6, \quad C_V = 3R, \quad C_P = 4R, \quad \gamma = \frac{4}{3} = 1.33$$
**Result**
$$\gamma_m > \gamma_d > \gamma_p$$
