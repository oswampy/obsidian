
**Work done by/on gas:**

- $W = -P_{\text{ext}} \Delta V$
-Expansion: $\Delta V = +ve \Rightarrow W = -ve$
- Work is done by gas, energy decreases

-Compression: $\Delta V = -ve \Rightarrow W = +ve$
- Work is done on gas, energy increases

#Derivation 
- Work done = $F \times s$
- $W = P \cdot A \times \Delta x$
- $W = -P \Delta V$ (where $A \times \Delta x = V$, $\Delta x = -ve$)

**Example:** Gas expands by $0.5 L$ against pressure of $1 \text{ atm}$ :

- $W = -P_{\text{ext}} \Delta V = -1 \times 0.5$
- $= -0.5 \times 101.3 J$ \[since $1 \text{ atm} \cdot L = 101.3 J$]
- $= -50.65 J$

#Law
## **Law of Conservation of Energy (1st Law):**

1. Energy cannot be created or destroyed, although one form can be converted into the other
    
2. The sum total of the energy of the universe remains constant or is conserved during any physical change or chemical reactions
    
3. Whenever a certain quantity of energy in one form disappears, an equivalent amount of energy in another form appears
    
4. It is impossible to create a perpetual motion machine, which perform work without consuming energy


**Mathematical Representation:** $$\Delta U = q + w$$

**Sign convention:**

- $q$ is $+ve$ if system absorbs energy
- $q$ is $-ve$ if system releases energy
- $w$ is $+ve$ if work done on system
- $w$ is $-ve$ if work done by system

**$\Delta U$ is a state function**

#Law 
## **0th Law of Thermodynamics:**
The law states that "if two systems A and B are separately in thermal equilibrium with a third system, then they are also in equilibrium with each other. Actually there is thermal equilibrium if there is no net flow of heat between them when brought in contact"
