
Enthalpy of a system may be defined as the sum of internal energy and pressure volume: **H = U + PV**

We cannot calculate H directly, only ΔH

## Change in Enthalpy

**ΔH = Hp - Hr**

- ΔH is +ve if Hp > Hr (endothermic)
- ΔH is -ve if Hr > Hp (exothermic)

## Relation between ΔH and ΔU

**ΔH = ΔU + PΔV**

But ΔU is significant only in gaseous because in solids and liquids ΔV ≈ 0, so new

- $PV_{r} = nrRT, PV_{p} = npRT$ (reactant and products)
- ∴ PΔV = Δng RT

Putting in first equation: **ΔH = ΔU + ΔngRT**

(Δng: No. of gaseous product moles - no. of gaseous reactant moles)

e.g. N₂ + 3H₂ → 2NH₃ (2 moles on product and 4 on reactant,Δng = -2)

### Exothermic:

N₂(g) + 3H₂(g) → 2NH₃(g) + 92.3J

Hp < Hr (-ve)

- Heat evolved is in positive sign
- Change in enthalpy (ΔH) is negative

### Endothermic:

N₂(g) + O₂(g) → 2NO(g) - 180 kJ

Hr < Hp (+ve)

- Heat absorbed is given negative sign
- Change in enthalpy (ΔH) is positive

## Bond Energy Concept

**If energy required in breaking the bond is more than energy released in bond making, ΔH is +ve, endothermic**

Example: HCl Formation

H-H(g) + Cl-Cl(g) → 2H-Cl(g)

Breaking bonds:

- H-H → H(g) + H(g)
- Cl-Cl → Cl(g) + Cl(g)

Formation:

- H-Cl(g) H-Cl(g)

**Energy calculations:**

- Energy required to break H-H bond = 433 kJ/mol
- Energy required to break Cl-Cl = 244 kJ/mol
- Energy released in formation of H-Cl = 2×431 kJ/mol

**ΔH = \[433 + 244] - \[2 × 431]** **= -185 kJ (endothermic)**

# Hess's Law
#Law 
The heat energy or enthalpy change in a physical process or chemical reaction is independent of the manner in which the change has been carried, i.e., single step or multi-step.

**Q)** Calculate $\Delta_f H$ of CO from

(i) $C(s) + O_2(g) \rightarrow CO_2(g), \ \Delta H = -393.5 , \text{kJ}$  
(ii) $CO(g) + \tfrac{1}{2}O_2(g) \rightarrow CO_2(g), \ \Delta H = -282.8 , \text{kJ}$

**A)** Make 3rd equation: $C + \tfrac{1}{2}O_2 \rightarrow CO$

Reverse (ii):  
$CO_2 \rightarrow CO + \tfrac{1}{2}O_2 \quad (\Delta H = +282.8 , \text{kJ})$

Add (i) and reverse (ii) and cancel opposite sides:  
$\Delta H = -393.5 + 282.8 = -110.7 , \text{kJ}$

Final equation:  
$C + \tfrac{1}{2}O_2 \rightarrow CO, \ \Delta H = -110.7 , \text{kJ}$

**Enthalpy of Reaction**

$A\to B$
$\Delta H_{r}= H_{B}-H_{A}$
$2A\to 3B$
$\Delta H_{r}= 3H_{B}-2H_{A}$
$\Delta H_{r = \sum aH_{products}-bH_{reactants}}$

**Bond Enthalpy**
amt of energy required to break one mole of bonds of a particular type between atoms in gaseous phase

$\Delta H_{r = \sum a\Delta _{_{bond}}H_{reactants}-b\Delta _{bond}H_{products}}$


**Enthalpy of Combustion**

$\Delta H_{r = \sum a\Delta _{_{c}}H_{reactants}-b\Delta _{c}H_{products}}$
