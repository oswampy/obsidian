

Entropy is a measure of degree of randomness or disorder of system.

**ΔS = S_final - S_initial**

- Entropy is a state function
- Entropy increases with temperature
-  Change in entropy in a reversible process is 0
**ΔS = ΔH/T** (For a reversible isothermal process)

SI unit - Joules per Kelvin per mole (JK⁻¹mol⁻¹)

## Factors Influencing Entropy

For a process to be spontaneous, entropy needs to be positive.

### Factors influencing Entropy:

1. **Volume** - V↑ S↑
2. **Temperature** - T↑ S↑
3. **Physical state** - most in gas, least in solids
4. **No. of particles** - ↑no. of particles ↑S

## GIBBS Free Energy (G)

Amount of energy available for doing useful work under conditions of constant temperature.

G is not useful but ΔG is useful in predicting direction of spontaneity.

Assume initial state: **G₁ = H₁ - TS₁** Assume final state: **G₂ = H₂ - TS₂**

**Change in G (ΔG) = (G₂ - G₁) = (H₂ - H₁) - (S₂ - S₁)T**

**ΔG = ΔH - TΔS** (Gibbs Helmholtz equation)

**$ΔG^∘=ΔH^∘−TΔS^∘$**(in Terms of Standard Free Energy of Formation)

If multiple values of ΔG∘\Delta G^\circΔG∘ are given:

$ΔG^∘=∑Δ_{f}G_{product}−∑Δ_{f}G_{reactant}$

- Standard free energy of formation of all free elements is assumed to be zero

### Physical significance of Gibbs Free change

$w =$ work done; two types are work expansion $(P\delta V)$ and work non-expansion

$\Delta H = \Delta U + P\Delta V$

$q = \Delta U + w$

For a reversible change taking place:

$\Delta S = \frac{q_{\text{rev}}}{T}$, $q_{\text{rev}} = T\Delta S$

$\therefore T\Delta S = \Delta H + W_{\text{non-expansion}}$

$\Delta H - T\Delta S = -W_{\text{non expansion}}$

$-\Delta G = W_{\text{non-expansion}}$

- Decrease in free energy of a system in a process is the measure of maximum useful work done by the system.

In batteries, $E =$ emf of cell, $n =$ no. of moles of electrons

$-G^\circ = nFE^\circ_{\text{cell}}$

$G^\circ$, $E^\circ$ are considered at standard temperature (298 K) and pressure (1 atm)

## Second Law of Thermodynamics

i) All spontaneous processes or naturally occurring processes are thermodynamically irreversible

ii) Without the help of an external agency, a spontaneous process cannot be reversed

iii) The entropy of an isolated system must increase if it is to be spontaneous in a particular direction

- **ΔS_system > 0**

iv) In a non-isolated system, the total entropy of both the system and surrounding must increase or be positive

- **ΔS_system + ΔS_surrounding > 0**

v) The total entropy of the universe must tend to increase in a spontaneous process

- **ΔS_total = ΔS_system + ΔS_surrounding ≥ 0**
