

## 2) From Alkyl Halide (R-X)

**Examples:** CH₃-Cl, C₂H₅-Br

- **↳ Elimination Rxn**
- **↳ Dehydrohalogenation (-HX)**
- **↳ α - β elimination**

$$H-\underset{|\beta}{\overset{H}{C}}-\underset{|\ \alpha}{\overset{H}{C}}-H + \text{Alc KOH} \xrightarrow{\Delta} \text{KCl} + \text{H}_2\text{O} + \text{CH}_2=\text{CH}_2$$

(Byproducts indicated)

---

### Saytzeff's Rule Example:

$$\text{CH}_3-\overset{\beta_1}{\text{CH}_2}-\overset{\alpha}{\underset{|\text{Br}}{\text{CH}}}-\overset{\beta_2}{\text{CH}_3}$$

**i)** $$\xrightarrow[\Delta]{\text{Alc KOH}} \text{CH}_3-\text{CH}_2-\text{CH}=\text{CH}_2$$ **(But-1-ene) - Minor**

**ii)** $$\xrightarrow[\Delta]{\text{Alc KOH}} \text{CH}_3-\text{CH}=\text{CH}-\text{CH}_3$$ **(Hoffman product) - Major**

---

### Saytzeff's Rule (Saytzeff product)

If an alkyl halide can eliminate the H-X in 2 different ways, the alkene will be formed in **Major** in which C-atoms joined by double bond are more alkylated (more no. of attached alkyl groups).

### 4) Addition of Unsymmetrical Alkene

**Markovnikov Rule:**

During addition to unsymmetric Alkene, **-ve part** of adding molecule goes to double bonded C-atom which has **less no. of H-atom**, **+ve part** goes to C-atom which has **more H**.

$$\text{CH}_3-\text{CH}=\text{CH}_2 + \text{H}^+\text{Cl}^-$$
