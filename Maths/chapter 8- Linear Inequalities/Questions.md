## Q1:
A solution of 8% boric acid is to be diluted by adding $2$% boric acid solution to it. The resulting mixture is to be more than $4$% but less than $6$% boric acid. If we have $640$ liters of the $8$%solution, how many liters of the 2% solution will have to be added?

---

**Solution:**

Let $x$ = liters of $2$ solution to be added.
- Initial volume: $V_1 = 640$
- New total volume: $V_2 = 640 + x$
- Boric acid content: $8 \cdot 640 +  \cdot x$

- Final concentration condition:


$0.04(640+x) < 0.08 \cdot 640 + 0.02x < 0.06(640+x)$

**Step 1:** Left inequality

$0.08 \cdot 640 + 0.02x > 0.04(640+x)$
$51.2 + 0.02x > 25.6 + 0.04x$
$x < 1280$


**Step 2:** Right inequality

$0.08 \cdot 640 + 0.02x < 0.06(640+x)$
$x > 320$

$\boxed{320 < x < 1280}$
