# Summation of sine
$\sin(\alpha+\beta)+sin(\alpha+2\beta)+sin(\alpha+3\beta)\dots+sin(\alpha+(n-1)\beta)$

$$=\frac{\sin \frac{n\beta}{2}}{\sin \frac{\beta}{2}}*\sin\left[ \alpha+(n-1) \frac{\beta}{2} \right]$$
- All angles are in ap with a common difference of beta and n is number of terms

for cos:
$$\frac{\sin \frac{n\beta}{2}}{\sin \frac{\beta}{2}}*\cos\left[ \alpha+(n-1) \frac{\beta}{2} \right]$$


# Product of cosine

$\cos(\alpha)cos(2\alpha)cos(2^2\alpha)cos(2^{3}\alpha)\dots\cos(2^{n-1}\alpha)$
$$=\frac{\sin2^n\alpha}{2^n\sin \alpha}$$

## Range of trigonometric functions
derivation of method

let $f(\theta) = a\sin \theta+b\cos \theta$
when angles are same:
$f(\theta) = \sqrt{ a^2+b^2 }\left( \frac{a}{\sqrt{ a^2+b^2 }}\sin \theta+\frac{b}{\sqrt{ a^2+b^2 }}\cos \theta \right)$
let $\frac{b}{\sqrt{ a^2+b^2 }}$ be $\sin \alpha$ and $\frac{a}{\sqrt{ a^2+b^2 }}$ be $\cos \alpha$
$\implies \sqrt{ a^2+b^2 }\sin (\theta+\alpha)$
when $\theta+\alpha$ is max(1) then it will be $+\sqrt{ a^2+b^2 }$
when $\theta+\alpha$ is min(-1) then it will be $-\sqrt{ a^2+b^2 }$
