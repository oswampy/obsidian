
If ‘$n$’ is a rational number and $|x| < 1$ then:

$1 + nx + \frac{n(n-1)}{2!}x^2 + \frac{n(n-1)(n-2)}{3!}x^3 + \dots = (1+x)^n$

$(1-x)^n = 1 - nx + \frac{n(n-1)}{2!}x^2 - \frac{n(n-1)(n-2)}{3!}x^3\dots$

$(1+x)^{-n} = 1 - nx + \frac{n(n+1)}{2!}x^2 - \frac{n(n+1)(n+2)}{3!}x^3\dots$

$(1-x)^{-n} = 1 + nx + \frac{n(n+1)}{2!}x^2 + \frac{n(n+1)(n+2)}{3!}x^3\dots$

# Q) 5th term of $\left(7 + \frac{8y}{3}\right)^{\frac{7}{4}}$

A) Write in terms of $(1+x)^n$, take 7 common:

$7^{\frac{7}{4}} \left[ 1 + \frac{8y}{21} \right]^{\frac{7}{4}}$

We need $n^{\text{th}}$ term so numerator will have $(n-1)$ terms divided by $(n-1)!$

$T_5 = \frac{n(n-1)(n-2)(n-3)}{4!} x^4$

$= 7^{\frac{7}{4}} \left[ \frac{\frac{7}{4} \left(\frac{7}{4}-1\right) \left(\frac{7}{4}-2\right) \left(\frac{7}{4}-3\right)}{4!} \right] \left( \frac{8y}{21} \right)^4$

$= \frac{7^{\frac{7}{4}} \cdot 5}{24} \cdot \frac{16y^4}{21^3}$
