Q) $n$-th greatest term in expansion of $(3 - 5x)^{12}$ when $x = \frac{1}{5}$ 
A) $\frac{12}{1 + \frac{3}{-5x}} \ge r$ $\frac{12}{\frac{5x + 3}{-5x}}$ $\frac{12x + 5x}{5x + 3} \ge r$ $\frac{60x}{5x + 3} \ge r$ 
	At $x = \frac{1}{5}$: $2 \le r \le 3$
	 $T_3 = {}^{12}C_2 (3)^{10} (-5x)^2$ 
	 $T_4 = {}^{12}C_3 (3)^9 (-5x)^3$ 
	 Magnitude of both are equal, but $T_4$ is greater.
