
Middle term in expansion of $(2x+a)^n$ 
Total terms $= n+1$ 
• If $n$ is even, mid $= \left(\frac{n}{2}+1\right)^{th}$ term 

• If $n$ is odd, mid $= \left(\frac{n+1}{2}\right)^{th}$ term and $\left(\frac{n+3}{2}\right)^{th}$ 

**The largest binomial coefficient occurs in the middle.**

### Q) largest coefficient in $(1+x)^{19}$
A) n=19 (odd) so middle = $T_{10} \;an  d\;T_{11}$
	r=9 and 10
	$^{19}C_{10}\;an d \;^{19}C_{9}$ are largest coefficients
### Q) Middle term in $(1-3x+3x^2-x^3)^{2n}$
A)using identity
	$(1-x)^{6n}$
	n = 6n (even)
	total = 6n+1 terms
	middle term = 3n+1
	r=3n
	$^{6n}C_{3n}x^{3n}$ is middle term
### Q) middle term of $\left( \frac{x-1}{x} \right)^n(1-x)^n$
A) taking -1 common
	$(-1)^n \frac{(1-x)}{x^n} ^{2n}$
	total terms = 2n+1
	middle = n+1
	r=n
	$^{2n}C_{n}x^n$ (it will be +ve because of the $-1^n$ having different power from 2n)
