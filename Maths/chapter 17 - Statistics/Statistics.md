

## Variance

$\sigma^2 = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}(a_i - \bar{x})^2$

## Standard deviation

$\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}(a_i - \bar{x})^2}$

#Q
Mean and Sdeviation of 100 observations were calc as 40 and 5.1, by a student who took by mistake 50 instead of 46 for one observation. What is correct mean and SD

### A)

Incorrect mean $= \frac{\text{Incorrect } \sum x_i}{100}$

$4000 = \text{Incorrect } \sum x_i$

Correct $\sum x_i = 4000 - 50 + 46 = 3996$

correct mean $= 39.96$

### Alternate formula for S.D

$\sigma = \sqrt{(x_i^2) - (\bar{x}_i)^2}$

$\therefore$ SD $= \sqrt{\frac{\text{Incorrect } \sum x_i^2 - 40^2}{100}}$

$(5.1)^2 (600) \times 100 = \text{Incorrect } \sum x_i^2$

$= 162601$

Correct $\sum x_i^2 \Rightarrow 162601 - 50^2 + 46^2$

$\Rightarrow 162601 - 900$

$\Rightarrow 161701$


correct S.D $= \sqrt{\frac{correct (x_i)^2 }{100}- (39.9)^2}$

correct S.D $= \sqrt{1617.01 - 1592.01} = 5$
