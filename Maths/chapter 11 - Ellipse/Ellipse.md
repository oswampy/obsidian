$PS + PS' = \text{Length of major axis}$

eq^n of ellipse with $C(h,k) = \frac{(x-h)^2}{a^2} + \frac{(y-k)^2}{b^2} = 1$

$e = \sqrt{1 - \left(\frac{\text{semi Minor}}{\text{semi Major}}\right)^2}$

**Latus Rectum:**

$LL'(ae, b^2/a)$

$\rightarrow LL' = \frac{2b^2}{a}$

$LL'(ae, -b^2/a)$

**Derivation of Length of LR**
#Derivation 
taking coord of LR in ellipse

$\frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1$

$y^2 = (1-e^2)b^2$

$1-e^2 = \frac{b^2}{a^2}$

$y^2 = \frac{b^4}{a^2}$

$y = \pm\frac{b^2}{a}$

hence total length $= \frac{2b^2}{a}$

**Proving $SP + S'P = \text{major axis}$**

$SP = ePM \Rightarrow e\left(\frac{a}{e} - x_1\right) \Rightarrow a - ex_1$

$S'P = ePM \Rightarrow e\left(\frac{a}{e} + x_1\right) \Rightarrow a + ex_1$

Adding both $\rightarrow 2a$ or Major axis

If $y$ axis is major axis, $b>a$

$LR = \frac{2a^2}{b^2}$

$L = (be, a^2/b)$


**Q)** Arc is in form of ellipse. It is 8m wide and 2m high at centre. Find height of arch at a point 1.5m from end.

**A)** $2a = 8$, $a = 4$, $b = 2$

$$\frac{x^2}{16} + \frac{y^2}{4} = 1$$


$$y^2 = \frac{1 - x^2}{16}$$

$$y^2 = \frac{4 - x^2}{4}$$

$$y = \frac{\sqrt{9.75}}{2} = 1.56 \text{ m}$$
