$\frac{Final\; z}{Initial\; z}$ = magnitude $\times$ Euler exponent  


$\dfrac{z_3 - z_1}{z_2 - z_1} = \left| \dfrac{z_3 - z_1}{z_2 - z_1} \right| e^{i\alpha}$

Any vector $AC$ can be written as $z_3 - z_2$,  
where $z_1$ is axis of rotation. (it will come after the - in the eq^n)
