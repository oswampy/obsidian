$\cos \theta = \dfrac{a}{r} \quad \Rightarrow \quad a = r \cos \theta$

$\sin \theta = \dfrac{b}{r} \quad \Rightarrow \quad b = r \sin \theta$

So,
$z = r \cos \theta + i r \sin \theta$
$z = r(\cos \theta + i \sin \theta)$

**Questions**

### Q) polar form of $1 + \sqrt{3}i$

$\theta = \tan^{-1} \sqrt{3} = \dfrac{\pi}{3}$

b) Put in polar form: $1 + \sqrt{3}i$

$r = \sqrt{1^2 + (\sqrt{3})^2} = \sqrt{4} = 2$
So,
$1 + \sqrt{3}i = 2 \left( \dfrac{1}{2} + i\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \right)$

$= 2 \Big(\cos \dfrac{\pi}{3} + i \sin \dfrac{\pi}{3}\Big)$
