

If a line makes an angle $\theta$ where $0 \leq \theta \leq \pi$ with $x$-axis in $+ve$ direction, then $\theta$ is called inclination and $\tan \theta$ is slope

$(m)\tan 30 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}$
$(m)\tan 150 = \frac{-1}{\sqrt{3}}$

- Slope of $x$-axis is 0; any horizontal line
- Slope of $y$-axis is undefined; any vertical line

- If two lines $(l_1,l_2)$ are parallel, $m_1 = m_2$
- If two lines are perpendicular, $m_1m_2 = -1$

- If $\theta$ is the acute angle between lines having slope $m_1, m_2$

## Point-Slope Form

The equation of the line passing through $A(x_1,y_1)$ and having slope $m$ is

$$y - y_1 = m(x-x_1)$$

## Two points Form

writing $m$ as $\left(\frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}\right)$

$$y-y_1 = \frac{(y_2-y_1)}{(x_2-x_1)}(x-x_1)$$

## Slope intercept Form

- Portion cut by line on $x$-axis is $x$-intercept vice versa
  by default it is $y$ intercept
$$y = mx + c$$
- (where c is $y$-intercept)

## Intercept Form


$$\frac{x}{a} + \frac{y}{b} = 1$$

## General Form of a straight Line

$Ax + By + C = 0$ \[Equation of a line]
Put in slope intercept form
$By = -Ax - C$ \[B has to be =1, and divide by B]
$y = \frac{-A}{B}x - \frac{C}{B}$
$m = \frac{-A}{B}$
$y$-intercept $= \frac{-C}{B}$

- The equation of parallel lines is
  $\frac{a_1}{a_2} = \frac{b_1}{b_2} \neq \frac{c_1}{c_2}$

- Passing through origin: $ax + by = 0$
