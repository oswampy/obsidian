Locus - Locus is the set of all points satisfying the given set of consistent geometrical conditions - A geometrical condition is consistent if there is at least one point satisfying it. 
Ex- The set of points which are equidistant from two fixed points $A$ & $B$ is a locus called perpendicular bisector $PA = P_1B$ $PA = P_2B$ 
In Form of complex nos: If $|z| = |z - \bar{z}|$ then we can write $|z-z_1| = |z-z_2|$

Q) A point moves so that the algebraic sum of its distances from 2 given perpendicular axes is equal to a constant quantity $a$. Find its locus. 
A) $P(x,y)$ $x + y = a$ 
 Sum of squares of distances of a moving $(P)$ point: From the fixed two points $(a,0)$ and $(-a,0)$ is equal to constant $2c^2$ 
$PA = \sqrt{(a-x)^2+(y-0)^2}$ 
$PB = \sqrt{(-a-x)^2+(y-0)^2}$ 
$PA^2+PB^2 = 2c^2$ 
$2x^2 + 2a^2 + 2y^2 = 2c^2$
$x^2 + y^2 = c^2-a^2$ (eq of circle)
