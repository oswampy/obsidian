## **Collinearity**

Points $A$, $B$, and $C$ are collinear if $AB + BC = AC$  
OR if slope of $AB =$ slope of $BC$
OR if area of triangle = 0

**Example:** Show $A(0, -2)$, $B(-1, 1)$, and $C(-2, 4)$ are collinear
A)
Slope of $AB = \frac{1 - (-2)}{-1 - 0} = \frac{3}{-1} = -3$
Slope of $BC = \frac{4 - 1}{-2 - (-1)} = \frac{3}{-1} = -3$
Slope of $AC = \frac{4 - (-2)}{-2 - 0} = \frac{6}{-2} = -3$

Since all slopes are equal, points $A$, $B$, and $C$ are collinear.

## **Section Formula**

For internal division (point between other points):  
$(x, y) = \left( \frac{m_1x_2 + m_2x_1}{m_1 + m_2}, \frac{m_1y_2 + m_2y_1}{m_1 + m_2} \right)$

For external division (point outside other points):  
$(x, y) = \left( \frac{m_1x_2 - m_2x_1}{m_1 - m_2}, \frac{m_1y_2 - m_2y_1}{m_1 - m_2} \right)$

**Question:** Find the 4th vertex of a parallelogram whose other consecutive vertices are $A(-2, -1)$, $B(1, 0)$, and $C(4, 3)$

In a parallelogram, diagonals bisect each other.

O is the midpoint of AC:  
$O = \left( \frac{4 + (-2)}{2}, \frac{3 + (-1)}{2} \right) = (1, 1)$

Using midpoint formula again for BD:  
$\left( \frac{1 + x}{2}, \frac{0 + y}{2} \right) = (1, 1)$

Solving,  
$\frac{1 + x}{2} = 1 \Rightarrow x = 1$  
$\frac{0 + y}{2} = 1 \Rightarrow y = 2$

Therefore, $D(1, 2)$


### Harmonic conjugate

Harmonic conjugate: If P divides AB internally in the ratios m:n and Q divides AB externally in the ratios m:n then P and Q are said to be harmonic conjugate of eachother

$$\frac{2}{AB} = \frac{1}{AP} + \frac{1}{AQ}$$
