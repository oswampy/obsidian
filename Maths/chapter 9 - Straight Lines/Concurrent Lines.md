

When 3 or more than 3 lines pass through the same point they are concurrent

If $\Delta = 0$, lines are concurrent.

$$
\begin{vmatrix}
a_1 & b_1 & c_1 \\
a_2 & b_2 & c_2 \\
a_3 & b_3 & c_3
\end{vmatrix} = 0
$$

Example: Show straight lines $4x + 7y = 9$, $5x - 8y + 15 = 0$, and $9x - y = 6$ are concurrent

Using the determinant:

$$\begin{vmatrix}
4 & 7 & -9 \\
5 & -8 & 15 \\
9 & -1 & 6
\end{vmatrix}
\
$$
Expanding:
$4(-8 + 15) - 7(30 - 135) - 9(-5 + 72) \implies 735 - 735 = 0$
