An Arithmetic Progression (AP) is a sequence of numbers where the difference between any two consecutive terms is constant.

- first term is a
- common difference is d
- no of terms is n
- last term is $nth$ term or l
- $T_{nth} = a+(n-1)d$
- $S_{n} = \frac{n}{2} (2a+(n-1)d)$

-If $a_{1},a_{2},a_{3}..a_{n}$ are in AP then $a_{1}+a_{n}=a_{2}+a_{n-1}..=a_{r}+a_{r-n-1}$(r is any integer)

-if $nT_{n}=mT_{m}$
	$T_{m+n}=0$
	if 100 times the 100th term =and= 50 times the 50th term, 150th is 0

## Solved Examples
Q) if a,b,c,d,e are in A.P find val of a-4b+6c-4d+e
A) since they are all in ap-
	$\frac{b+d}{2} = c$
	$b+d = 2c$
	similarlily $a+e=2c$
	rearranging q: (a+e)-4(b+d)+6c
	$2c-8c+6c=0$

Q)Find 4 terms whose sum is 20 and sum of squares is 120
A) (a-3d)+(a-d)+(a+d)+(a+3d) = 20 $(i)$
	a = 5
	putting a in $(i)$
	$d=\pm{1}$
	$\implies{2},4,6,8(when\; d=1)$
	$\implies8,6,4,2(when\; d=-1)$

Q) sum of n terms is given by $Sn = 2n^{2}+n$ find the sequence
A)$T_{n} = S_{n}-S_{n-1}$
	$2n^{2}+n-2(n-1)^2+(n-1)$
	$6n-3$
	$\implies3,9,15,21$

A)adding any constant to each will keep the ap so we add 2 to simplify
	$\frac{a+b+c}{a}, \frac{a+b+c}{b}, \frac{a+b+c}{c}$
