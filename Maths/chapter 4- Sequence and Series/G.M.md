Suppose we insert n terms between a and b
$a,G_{1},G_{2},G_{3},G_{n},b$
if these are in G.P then these terms are called a **Geometric Mean**

-If a single G is inserted bw a and b the G is the Only G.M
	$a,G,b$ -> $\frac{G}{a}=\frac{b}{G}$->$\boxed{G=\sqrt{ ab }}$
	both a and b must have same signs otherwise we get complex roots
-For n terms inserted bw a and b:
	$a,G_{1},G_{2},G_{n},b$, b is the $T_{n+2}$th term
	$b=ar^{n+2-1}$
	$\boxed{r=\left( \frac{b}{a} \right)^{\frac{1}{n+1}}}$
	$G_{1}=a(\frac{b}{a})^{\frac{1}{n+1}}$
	$G_{n}=ar^n=a(\frac{b}{a})^{\frac{n}{n+1}}$

## Properties
If n GMs are inserted bw a and b, product of n GMs is nth power of a single GM
$G_{1}*G_{2}*G_{3}\dots G_{n}=(\sqrt{ ab })^n$

## Solved Examples

Q) insert 4 gms between 2 and 486
A) a = 2, b =486
	r = $(243)^ {\frac{1}{4+1}}$
	r=3
	$G_{1}=2*3=6$
	$G_{2}=2*9=18$
	$G_{3}=2*27=54$
	$G_{4}=2*81=162$

Q) if G be the G.M bw x and y prove
$\frac{1}{G^2-x^2}+\frac{1}{G^2-y^2}=\frac{1}{G^2}$
A)since only one GM , $G=\sqrt{ xy },G^2=xy$
	$\frac{1}{xy-x^2}+\frac{1}{xy-y^2}$
	$\frac{1}{y-x} \left[ \frac{1}{x}- \frac{1}{y} \right]$
	$\frac{1}{y-x} \left[ \frac{y-x}{xy} \right]$
	$\frac{1}{xy}$
