If a and be are two non zero numbers, then harmonic mean of them is the number H such that a,H,b is a H.P. Hence $\frac{1}{a}, \frac{1}{H}, \frac{1}{b}$ is in AP
for **one** H.M between two numbers:
$$H = \frac{2ab}{a+b}$$

---

## Solved Examples

Q) 4 HMS between 2/3 and 2/13
A) for HM qs just convert to AM and solve
	4 AMS between 3/2 and 13/2
	$d=\frac{10}{\frac{2}{4+1}}\implies d=1$
	$A_{1}=\frac{3}{2}+1\implies \frac{5}{2}$
	$A_{2}=\frac{5}{2}+1\implies \frac{7}{2}$
	$A_{3}=\frac{7}{2}+1\implies \frac{9}{2}$
	$A_{4}=\frac{9}{2}+1\implies \frac{11}{2}$
	Since we got the 4 AMs we just invert them for the 4 HMs:
	$\implies\frac{5}{2}, \frac{7}{2}, \frac{9}{2}, \frac{11}{2}$

Q) if H be the harmonic mean between p and q find $\frac{H}{q}+\frac{H}{p}$
	we know for one HM:
	$H=\frac{2pq}{p+q}$
	in q:
	$H\left[ \frac{1}{q}+\frac{1}{p} \right]$
	$\frac{2pq}{p+q}\left[ \frac{p+q}{pq} \right]$
	$={2}$
