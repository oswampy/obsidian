If $a_{1},a_{2},a_{3}$ are in A.P
then $\frac{1}{a_{1}}, \frac{1}{a_{2}}, \frac{1}{a_{3}}$ are in H.P
If a,b,c are in H.P
$\frac{1}{a} + \frac{1}{c}= \frac{2}{b}$
$\boxed{b = \frac{2ac}{a+c}}$

- $nth\; term \;of\; h.p= \frac{1}{nth\;term\;of\;ap}$

## Solved Examples
Q) $a_{8}= \frac{1}{2}$ and $a_{14}=\frac{1}{3}$ find the 20th term
A)$a+7d=2$ and$a+13d=3$ in H.P
	solving we get d = 1/6
	putting in eq we get a = 5/6
	$a_{20}=\frac{5}{6} + 19* \frac{1}{6}$ in AP
	$a_{20} = \frac{1}{4}$ In HP

Q) $mth$ term of HP is n and $nth$ term is m, prove its $rth$ term is $\frac{mn}{r}$
A)$\frac{1}{n}=a+(m-1)d$
	$\frac{1}{m}=a+(n-1)d$
	subtracting
	$\frac{1}{mn} = d$
	putting in any eq we get $a=\frac{1}{mn}$
	$rth\;term=\frac{1}{\frac{1+(r-1)}{mn}}$
	$rth \;term=\frac{mn}{r}$
