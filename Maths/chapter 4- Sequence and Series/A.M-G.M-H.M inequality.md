**Proof of A.M$\ge$GM** (with 2 terms: a and b)
	A = $\frac{a+b}{2}$
	G=$(\sqrt{ ab })$
	subtracting
	$A-G=\frac{a+b-2\sqrt{ ab }}{2}$
	$A-G=\frac{(\sqrt{ a }-\sqrt{ b })^{2}}{2}$
	since the min value of a perfect square is 0
	A will always be greater or equal to G

**Proof of $G^2=AH$** (with 2 terms: a and b)
	$G = \sqrt{ ab }$ so $G^2=ab$
	$H=\frac{2ab}{a+b}$
	$A=\frac{a+b}{2}$
	multiply A and H we get ab
	$\therefore G^2=AH$
